**9th Grade.** The entry point into full participation in the life of the church.

Let's be very clear about what Confirmation is — and is not:

- It is **not** graduation from church.
- It is **not** a gold star for attending.
- It is **not** a hoop to jump through so parents get the photo.

It **is** three things, all at once:

1. **God confirms** a youth's baptismal identity as a beloved child of God.
2. **The youth confirms** their faith in Jesus Christ — not their parents' faith, not their grandmother's faith, their own.
3. **Everyone confirms** that this is the beginning, not the end.

{{< callout type="important" >}}
Confirmation works best when it's **relational, not informational.** A student will forget most of the doctrine by Thanksgiving. They won't forget the adult who knew their name, asked their hard questions, and prayed for them by name on Confirmation Sunday.
{{< /callout >}}

- **What Do We Do Now?** — Worship, Word, sacraments, and service as a way of life
