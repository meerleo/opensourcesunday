**Sent after Session 3 · Sin.**

---

> Hello Parents,
>
> Please join us next Sunday for a combined parent/student class.
>
> **Recap:** We have just finished up unit three, where we discussed a central doctrine of the Christian faith — the doctrine of **sin.**
>
> In **Matthew 22:34–40,** Jesus reminds us that humanity is meant to love God and love one another. Sin is both the *power* in the world and the *practices* in our lives that get in the way of those two purposes.
>
> Sin manifests individually as well as corporately. As Christians, we recognize that our world is deeply affected by sin — but we also understand that by the power of Jesus' resurrection, **God has defeated sin** and is in the process of making all things new. In the meantime, we are called to **repent** — turn from sin — and join God in God's work of loving redemption.
>
> **Your turn:** This week we encourage you to read **Matthew 22:34–40** with your child.
>
> - Share where you have seen your family love God and love others well.
> - Share where you have seen your family *not* do this well — confess and repent together. How can you do better?
> - Invite your child to share their own thoughts.
> - Close by praying together that God would help your family turn from sin and grow in love.
>
> Even just taking ten minutes out of your day to engage with your child in this way is incredibly important in helping them develop a faith that lasts.
