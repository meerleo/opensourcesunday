> **Recap:** We just finished up unit six, where we discussed the question: **What does it mean to be a disciple of Christ?**
>
> Being a disciple of Jesus leads to abundant and eternal life — but it also means sacrifice. While we find security in our relationship with God and with the people of God, we are also called to take risks. By standing against evil and standing up for God's love in the world, we often sacrifice our own comfort and we risk being denied by the world. Being a disciple means to be a *student* or a *follower* of Jesus — the same Jesus who sacrificed it all for us.
>
> **Your turn:** Read **Mark 8:31–38** with your children and ask:
>
> - When does loving God and loving our neighbors call us to take risks?
> - What is something our family could sacrifice for the good of others?
> - What do you think Jesus means by: *"For whoever wants to save their life will lose it, but whoever loses their life for me and for the gospel will save it"* (v.35)?
>
> **Out-of-the-box idea:** Watch *The Karate Kid* (1984, PG) and discuss the mentor-disciple relationship between Daniel LaRusso and Mr. Miyagi. Describe the character of Mr. Miyagi and his authority. Then discuss: **how is this relationship like — and different from — our relationship with Jesus?**
