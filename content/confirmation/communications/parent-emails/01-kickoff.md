>
> **Recap:** The idea of a **covenant** is a central theme in the Bible. We can think about a covenant as *a promise with the intent of relationship.* God is the initiator of his covenant of love with humanity, and in Jesus Christ he is also the fulfiller of that covenant. We have a God who both pursues us, *and* refuses to let us go. In response to that great love, we are then invited to participate in God's covenant by loving him and loving our neighbor.
>
> The confirmation journey is a covenant between **God, students and their families, and the church.** God continues to grow and nurture; students and their families lean into following Jesus; the church supports and surrounds those getting confirmed.
>
> Therefore, Confirmation requires the following commitments:
>
> - Attending class every week, or communicating when there will be an absence
> - Attending church on Sundays
> - Engaging in "Home Practices"
> - Being honest
>
> If these commitments are not reasonably met, the student may not be able to get confirmed. If you are feeling challenged by this, let's talk.
>
> **Home Practices this week:**
>
> - Read **Deuteronomy 7:9** with your confirmand and ask:
>   - What does it mean that God is faithful to his covenant with us? *(He will always love us; he will hold us accountable and still fulfill it when we need grace or forgiveness; he will always be with us, etc.)*
>   - How do we respond to that covenant faithfully?
> - **Write a covenant together** regarding the student's commitment to their faith journey and your commitment to them throughout that journey *(see attached).*
>
> Begin by asking/answering these three questions together:
>
> - What do you hope to learn / how do you want to grow during this journey?
> - In what ways do you want to practice your faith during Confirmation?
> - How do you want our family to support you in Confirmation?
>
> Parents, you are **not** expected to know all the answers. Investing even just a few intentional minutes to engage with your child's faith journey is priceless.
