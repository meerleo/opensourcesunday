> Hello Parents,
>
> **Important Dates:**
>
> - Next week, **3/8,** is a parent class.
>
> **Recap:** Last week we answered the big question: **Will you devote yourself to the breaking of bread?**
>
> When we think of breaking bread together, we immediately think of **Communion.** Communion has past, present, and future implications. At communion we **remember** Jesus' sacrifice for us on the cross — a sacrifice that reminds us of Passover, when God saved and delivered the Hebrew people from slavery through the blood of the lamb. At communion we **receive** God's grace and are spiritually nourished by the Spirit of Christ present in the bread and the cup. At communion we **see a foreshadowing** of the great heavenly banquet table that will be hosted by Christ in eternity.
>
> One of the most basic spiritual practices is to simply **share a meal with other followers of Jesus.** At the same time, we are called to welcome **strangers** to our own tables. As Jesus extended hospitality to us, we are called to extend hospitality to others. We make room at our tables for people different from us, because there is always room at the table of Christ.
>
> **Your turn:** Read **Luke 22:14–23** together and ask:
>
> - Why do we celebrate Communion / the Lord's Supper? *(To remember Jesus' sacrifice, to experience Jesus' Spirit now, and to anticipate the great heavenly banquet feast with Jesus in the future.)*
> - Who is invited to Jesus' table? *(Everyone.)*
> - Who would you like to invite to eat with us at our table?
>
> Take a look at your calendar. Take an inventory of how often your family is eating dinner together:
>
> - How can you increase this essential family- and faith-forming practice?
> - How can you make it even more intentionally centered around Jesus — and his example of sharing the table as a means to faith and relationship?
