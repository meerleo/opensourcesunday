**For each mentor.**

Every confirmand gets an adult mentor for the year — someone from the congregation who commits to meeting with, praying for, and eventually speaking a blessing over them at the Confirmation Dessert. Mentors are the single most underrated part of a Confirmation year.

{{< callout type="warning" >}}
**We use the published *Big God, Big Questions* [Home / Mentor Guide](https://www.pcusastore.com/Products/1571532412/big-god-big-questions-home-mentor-guide.aspx)** by Geneva Press. That resource is copyrighted and not reproduced on this site — give each mentor a copy. What follows is **our own church-specific guidance** on picking and supporting mentors.
{{< /callout >}}

## Selecting mentors

From the [Big God, Big Questions Teacher's Guide](../teachers-guide/), adapted:

- A mentor needs to be **accessible** to their confirmand on a regular basis.
- Except for unique circumstances, connect confirmands to mentors who are **not members of their own family.**
- Take a young person's **pastoral needs** into account when pairing.
- The most important qualifications for a mentor are **not piety or orthodoxy, but love and grace.**
- Every adult who works with children and youth must be screened through a **criminal background check.** Contact your congregation's insurance representative.
- Check with your pastor or Christian educator about your church's **safe-practices policy** for youth. Meeting in a coffee shop or at church when others are present is usually the right default.

## What mentors commit to

- **Attending the sessions where mentors are expected:** Session 1 (Kickoff / Covenant), Session 4 (Lord), Session 8 (Teaching), and Session 12 (Crossing Over). The session pages flag these.
- **A monthly check-in** with their confirmand — a coffee, a walk, or a shared activity. No agenda required beyond "how's your faith going?"
- **Praying for their confirmand** by name, every day, all year.
- **Speaking a blessing** over their confirmand at the [Confirmation Dessert](../../events/confirmation-dessert/).

## Monthly rhythm

A simple pattern to share with mentors:

- **Early fall** — introduce yourself. Find out what they like to do. Let them know what you're praying for them about.
- **Mid-year** — walk through the Big God, Big Questions mentor-guide prompt for the current session. Listen more than you talk.
- **Lent / spring** — check in on the statement of faith. Be a safe person to read the first, terrified draft.
- **May** — write your blessing for the Dessert. Show up. Mean it.

## Supporting mentors

Mentor burnout is real. The lead teacher should:

- Text mentors the week of each Confirmation class they're expected at (don't assume the calendar is enough).
- Email mentors a 2-sentence summary after each session so they can bring it up naturally in their next check-in.
- Check in with mentors by phone twice a year. Some need encouragement; some need to gracefully tag out.
