**A full-year Confirmation schedule.**

This is the master calendar we use. It covers every class session, the fall retreat, Confirmation Dessert, Confirmation Sunday, and the parent touchpoints in between. Copy it, shift the dates, and adapt to your own program.

## Typical shape of a year

- **September** — invites go out to rising 9th graders; Kickoff (Session 1A)
- **Early October** — first parent/student class (Session 1B Covenant)
- **October–November** — Sessions 2 (Trust) and 3 (Sin)
- **Mid-November** — **Fall Retreat** (Friday–Sunday)
- **December** — Sessions 4 (Lord) and 5 (Salvation), with a break for Christmas
- **January–February** — Sessions 6 (Disciple), 7 (Love)
- **March** — Session 8 (Teaching), **Serve Saturday,** Session 9 (Fellowship)
- **Early April** — Holy Week reenactment; break for Easter
- **April–May** — Sessions 10 (Breaking Bread), 11 (Prayer); statement-of-faith drafting
- **Late May** — Session 12 (Crossing Over); **Confirmation Dessert**
- **End of May / early June** — **Confirmation Sunday** (Children & Youth Sunday)

## Planning tips

- **Sync with your church's master calendar** before you commit to dates. Mission trips, Holy Week events, and vacation-week breaks will eat your Sunday mornings.
- **Build in flex weeks.** Snow days, graduations, and unexpected illnesses will cost you a session or two. Having 2–3 "no class" weeks scattered through the year gives you room.
- **Pair each class with its [parent email](../../communications/parent-emails/).** Send the email the week after, while the topic is fresh.
- **Put the fall retreat before Session 4.** The retreat bonds the class; after it, the deep-cut sessions (Lord, Salvation, Disciple) land differently.
