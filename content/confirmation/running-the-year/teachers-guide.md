## The arc at a glance

Twelve sessions, anchored in the **four Big Questions** asked at a PCUSA Confirmation:

1. Trusting in the gracious mercy of God, do you turn from the ways of sin and renounce evil and its power in the world?
2. Who is your Lord and Savior?
3. Will you be Christ's faithful disciple, obeying his Word and showing his love?
4. Will you devote yourself to the church's teaching and fellowship, to the breaking of bread and the prayers?

The twelve sessions map to those vows:

1. **Covenant** — Joshua 24
2. **Trust** — Psalm 105; Hebrews 11
3. **Sin** — Matthew 22:34–40; Genesis 3
4. **Lord** — Philippians 2; Exodus 3; 1 Kings 18
5. **Salvation** — Ephesians 2; Exodus 3; Luke 19; 2 Kings 5; Mark 2; Matthew 18; Luke 15
6. **Disciple** — Mark 8; Mark 12; Matthew 20; Acts 7
7. **Love** — John 21
8. **Teaching** — 2 Timothy 1; Hebrews 12
9. **Fellowship** — 1 Corinthians 12; John 13
10. **Breaking Bread** — Luke 22; Exodus 12; Acts 10
11. **Prayer** — Luke 18; Colossians 4; 1 Thessalonians 5; Psalm 51; Romans 5
12. **Crossing Over** — Joshua 3; Romans 6

## Pacing

Most sessions are split into a **Part A** and **Part B,** taught across two class meetings two weeks apart. A handful (9, 10, 12) are single meetings. All told, expect ~22 class meetings plus a fall retreat, Confirmation Dessert, and Confirmation Sunday.

See the [Sample Calendar](../sample-calendar/) for a concrete year's worth of dates.

## How to prep each session

Each [session page](../../sessions/) on this site follows the actual flow a teacher works through:

- **Before class** — supplies, setup, guests to invite
- **Opening / icebreaker** — warm up the room
- **Read** — the scripture + context you teach around
- **Discuss** — the questions with answer hints for the teacher
- **Activity** — the hands-on or group piece
- **Close** — how to send them out

Read the page a few days ahead so you know what props to gather (clay, bread, bowls of water, a 2×4, the right video cued up). Many sessions have optional extensions you can pull in if discussion runs short.

## Working with mentors

Every confirmand gets an adult mentor. Hand each mentor the [Mentor's Guide](../mentors-guide/) at the start of the year. The session pages flag where mentors are expected (Session 1, Session 4, Session 8, Session 12) — make sure those dates are on mentors' calendars well in advance.

## Working with parents

The rhythm of [parent emails](../../communications/parent-emails/) runs parallel to the sessions. Send the matching email the week after each topic. It gives parents a Home Practice to try with their confirmand and keeps the dinner-table theology going between classes.
