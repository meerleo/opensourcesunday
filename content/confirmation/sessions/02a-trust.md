**Part A of Session 2 · Trust.**

**Overview.** When God calls us into covenant relationship, God calls us to trust. When we understand how God has acted in the past, is acting in the present, and promises to act tomorrow, we can trust in God's promises even in the face of uncertainty. **When we come to know God, we come to trust God.**

**The big question for the next two weeks:** *How do we know and trust God?*

{{< callout type="info" >}}
**Two ways to run this session.** Source material includes two complete variants — pick the one that fits your space and weather.

- **Outdoor (recommended):** Start with a blindfolded trust walk in a park, then teach the psalm. Song (*Oceans*) moves to Session 2B.
- **Indoor:** Skip the trust walk, go straight into discussion, and include *Oceans* in this session.

Both variants are laid out below. Sections marked *"Outdoor only"* or *"Indoor only"* are variant-specific; everything else is shared.
{{< /callout >}}

## Before class

- Prep teachers with the testimony questions (see below)
- Note cards for questions at the end
- Print song lyrics (*Oceans* by Hillsong United) — indoor variant
- Bluetooth speaker — indoor variant
- Blindfolds, blankets, two Bibles — outdoor variant
- **Text confirmands in advance:** dress warmly, wear sneakers — outdoor variant

## Icebreaker

**Outdoor only — the trust walk:**

Blindfolded walk through the park where first the partner guide can *touch* them, then only their *voice,* and they are spun around at various times.

**Ask:**

- What was hard about that?
- Where did trust come in?
- How was that walk like following God?

**Both variants — go-around:**

- Name
- Where you fall in the birth order of your siblings
  - What's cool about that?
  - What's not so cool about that?

**Indoor only — add:**

- Who do you trust most in the world? Why?
- What does it take to build trust? To lose it?

## Ground Rules

**Say:**

> First, we want to create a circle of trust here, so we want to come up with some ground rules — a covenant we want to make with one another.

Examples:

- No question is a dumb question
- Everyone has to pray at least once (last one in the room has to pray)
- Listen louder than you speak
- What happens here stays here — unless it's a danger to you or someone else
- Three put-ups for a put-down
- No cursing

**Get the group chat going** (if it's not already running from Session 1 — see [Group Chat Setup](../../running-the-year/group-chat/)).

## Teacher Testimony

**Say:**

> We hope you can trust us teachers — but now we want to talk about what it looks like to trust God. First, we'll share a little bit of our own faith journey.

*Have teachers share openly and briefly on these questions:*

- **Describe what it looks and feels like to know and trust God.**
- **When did you have a tough time trusting God?**
- **How did you come to know and trust God?** (What things have helped you be close to God / know God?)

## Discussion — Knowing and Trusting

**Say:**

> Trust always comes through relationships. Just like with anyone, before we can trust God, we have to know God.

**Ask:**

- Who do you know best in the world?
  - How do you know them best?
- How *can* you know someone? *(You spend time with them now. You look at the patterns they've shown in the past.)*

## Read — Psalm 105

**Say:**

> The Bible is one of the ways we can know who God is — especially by looking at who God has been in the past.
>
> The book of Psalms is a book of songs and poems used in the worship life of God's people, from the Old Testament through today. It's been called *"a Bible within the Bible"* because there's so much in it — joy, sadness, anger, the past, the present, the future. Today we'll look at one of the psalms that sings of the past.

*Ask volunteers to read aloud from Psalm 105:*

- **Abraham and Sarah** — Psalm 105:7–11
- **Joseph** — Psalm 105:16–22 *(outdoor variant)* or **Psalm 105:16–25** *(indoor variant)*
- **Moses and the exodus** — Psalm 105:37–41 *(outdoor)* or **Psalm 105:26–36** *(indoor)*

**Ask:**

- What does the psalm tell you about God being trustworthy? Gracious? Merciful?

**Say:**

> Knowing how someone has typically acted in the past is helpful — but does that mean we *fully* know them?
>
> We have to have relationship with them *now.*

*(Outdoor variant:)* How can we do that?

**Ask:**

- How do you call upon the name of God?
- How does your soul rest in God's embrace?
- How do we invite the Spirit to lead us? To be in the presence of our Savior?

## The Promises of God

**Say:**

> As we come to trust God, we come to be able to trust in the *promises* of God.

**One current promise of God:**

> ***Matthew 28:20b — "And remember, I am with you always, to the end of the age."***

**One future promise of God:**

> ***Revelation 21:4 — "He will wipe every tear from their eyes. Death will be no more; mourning and crying and pain will be no more, for the first things have passed away."***

## Close

**Say:**

> The journey of faith is about more and more deeply *trusting* God — and so it's about more and more deeply *knowing* God through building relationship with him.

**Close with worship.** Ask a volunteer (or rotate through the circle) to read **Psalm 105:1–5** one line at a time.
