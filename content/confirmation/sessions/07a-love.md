**Part A of Session 7 · Love.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules; invite someone to pray
- Print copies of **Resource 7a** (the phileo vs. agape sheet) — enough for everyone or partners
- Print copies of John 21:15–19 (narrator / Jesus / Peter roles)
- Mural paper or large whiteboard + markers

**Big question for the next two weeks:**

> ***Will you be Christ's faithful disciple, obeying his Word and showing his love?***

## Opener

**Ask:** What does it mean to **show Christ's love?**

**Brainstorm** synonyms for the word *love* — affection, adore, fondness, tenderness, devotion. Push the group to think more tangibly about *acts and experiences* of love.

**Optional game: Last LOVE standing** — go around the room; each person names a kind of love without repeating. Last one standing wins.

## Phileo vs. Agape

Hand out copies of **Resource 7a.** Review the differences between **phileo** (brotherly love, mutual affection) and **agape** (sacrificial love). Work on the sheet together, helping one another translate the words for *love* in each Bible passage.

Return to the brainstormed *love* list on the mural paper. Ask participants to choose the synonyms and expressions of love that most closely resemble **phileo** and **agape.**

## Read — John 21:15–19

**Context:** This is **after the resurrection.** Jesus has appeared to the disciples on the beach. Peter — the one who denied Jesus three times before the crucifixion — is about to be asked a question three times.

*Invite volunteers to read the parts of Jesus, Peter, and a narrator.*

> ***John 21:15–19 — When they had finished eating, Jesus said to Simon Peter, "Simon son of John, do you love me more than these?"***
>
> ***"Yes, Lord," he said, "you know that I love you."***
>
> ***Jesus said, "Feed my lambs."***
>
> ***Again Jesus said, "Simon son of John, do you love me?"***
>
> ***He answered, "Yes, Lord, you know that I love you."***
>
> ***Jesus said, "Take care of my sheep."***
>
> ***The third time he said to him, "Simon son of John, do you love me?"***
>
> ***Peter was hurt because Jesus asked him the third time, "Do you love me?" He said, "Lord, you know all things; you know that I love you."***
>
> ***Jesus said, "Feed my sheep. Very truly I tell you, when you were younger you dressed yourself and went where you wanted; but when you are old you will stretch out your hands, and someone else will dress you and lead you where you do not want to go."*** *(Jesus said this to indicate the kind of death by which Peter would glorify God.)* ***Then he said to him, "Follow me!"***

### Ask — at tables

- Why do you think Jesus asked Simon Peter if he loved him?
- Jesus asked Peter if he loved *(agape)* him. Why do you think Peter responded, *"I love (phileo) you"*?
- If this were the only Bible story we had about love, how would you define **Christian love?**

### Ask — in big group

- What is the connection between loving Jesus and feeding his sheep?
- Whom do *sheep* represent in the story?
- Who are Jesus' sheep today?
- What actions constitute *"feeding"* them?

## Application — Four Ways to Love

At tables, have each person name:

1. One way you can show **phileo** *at home.*
2. One way you can show **phileo** *outside of the home* (at work, school, somewhere else).
3. One way you can show **agape** *at home.*
4. One way you can show **agape** *outside of the home.*

## Close — We Love Because He First Loved Us

> ***1 John 4:19 — "We love because he first loved us."***

Encourage the confirmands to think of ways they are both **sheep** *and* **feeders** — so they see themselves as *contributors* as well as *recipients.*

**Close in prayer.**
