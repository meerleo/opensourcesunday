**Part A of Session 3 · Sin.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules posted; **collect phones**
- Target + something soft to throw at it (ping-pong balls, rolled socks, a small ball)
- Screen or laptop ready for the BibleProject video
- Bibles for all

## Opening

**Ask:**

- What are the sort of things that break relationships?
- When you hear the word *sin*, what comes to mind?

## Activity — Missing the Mark

Have students take turns throwing the object at a difficult target — far enough away that most of them will miss.

**Ask:**

- Was it difficult to hit the mark? Why?
- What if you were to practice?
- What if you had a coach or a guide?

## Video — BibleProject on Sin (*Khata*)

Play the BibleProject short video on the Hebrew word for sin, *khata* (to miss the mark): <https://bibleproject.com/explore/video/khata-sin/>

**Ask:**

- Did anything jump out to you about the video?
- What do you think it means that every hero of the Bible sinned — except Jesus?
  - It means God *expects* us to miss the mark, and loves us anyway.
  - God uses imperfect people to do extraordinary things.
  - Forgiveness is always available.

## Read — Matthew 22:34–40

> ***Matthew 22:34–40 — Hearing that Jesus had silenced the Sadducees, the Pharisees got together. One of them, an expert in the law, tested him with this question: "Teacher, which is the greatest commandment in the Law?"***
>
> ***Jesus replied: "'Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind.' This is the first and greatest commandment. And the second is like it: 'Love your neighbor as yourself.' All the Law and the Prophets hang on these two commandments."***

**Ask:**

- Who is in the passage?
- Who can give a summary of what happened?
- What relationships do the greatest commandments talk about?

**Draw or reference a cross:** there's our **vertical** relationship with God and our **horizontal** relationship with people.

**Say:**

> Sin always means **broken relationships.** We miss the mark when we don't love God and neighbor.

**Ask:** Does anyone do these things perfectly? No — we all miss the mark.

## What Causes Sin?

### Read — Genesis 3:1–6

> ***Genesis 3:1–6 — Now the serpent was more crafty than any of the wild animals the Lord God had made. He said to the woman, "Did God really say, 'You must not eat from any tree in the garden'?"***
>
> ***The woman said to the serpent, "We may eat fruit from the trees in the garden, but God did say, 'You must not eat fruit from the tree that is in the middle of the garden, and you must not touch it, or you will die.'"***
>
> ***"You will not certainly die," the serpent said to the woman. "For God knows that when you eat from it your eyes will be opened, and you will be like God, knowing good and evil."***
>
> ***When the woman saw that the fruit of the tree was good for food and pleasing to the eye, and also desirable for gaining wisdom, she took some and ate it. She also gave some to her husband, who was with her, and he ate it.***

### Little-s sin vs. Big-S Sin

**Say:**

> There are the **little-s sins** we commit and the **big-S Sin** that is a *power* — a power that causes sins and separation from God.
>
> The Bible talks about big-S Sin as caused by three things:

1. **Satan** — symbolized by the snake in many traditions. Supernatural powers of evil that try to lie and distort things.
2. **Flesh** — our own misled desires or even thoughts.
3. **The World** — the influence of others around us.

We'll continue with *our response* to sin next week.
