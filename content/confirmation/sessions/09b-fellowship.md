**Part B of Session 9 · Fellowship.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules; choose someone to pray
- Whiteboard + markers
- Note cards for new metaphors
- Pens
- Printed reading-closing handouts for everyone
- **Children & Youth Sunday template** (see [events/children-and-youth-sunday](../../events/children-and-youth-sunday/))

## Recap

**Big question:** *Will you devote yourself to the church's fellowship?*

**Ask:** Does anyone remember what *koinonia* means?

> *Fellowship. Sharing in a common bond. Action is always included — contribution, sharing, participation. Not just being together, but doing together. Doing life together.*

## Body Parts Debate

Have a volunteer draw a simple sketch of each of the following on the whiteboard: **hand, foot, head, eyes, ears, nose.**

Ask participants to write and / or draw the important things done by that part of the body.

Then invite volunteers to pretend to *be* a body part in a skit, two at a time. Have them **argue with one another over who is the most important part of the body.**

- 20 seconds to say why *they* are the better body part
- 20 seconds for the opposing body part
- 10-second rebuttals each

Do 3–4 different match-ups.

## Gifts Inventory

Have youth open BIG Journal to pages 82–83. Read the instructions together, then allow a few quiet moments for confirmands to mark their responses.

**After they finish, discuss:**

- How did you feel as you completed the gifts inventory?
- What stands out for you about your list?
- How did you feel last week when people spoke about your gifts?
- In what ways can your gifts contribute to the unity and mission of the church?

*(Mission = to restore the world through sacrificial love.)*

## Read — 1 Corinthians 12:12–27

**Introduce:**

> The church in Corinth was having trouble remembering the *koinonia* — the fellowship — that God intended for them. So Paul used this analogy.

*Read* **1 Corinthians 12:12–27.**

**Discuss:**

- According to the passage, why does the body need to be made up of different members? *(To contribute something unique and help the whole function — v.17.)*
- If we are one body, what does that mean about the church as a community? *(Interdependent. We're all connected and need each other. There is no following Jesus apart from the church.)*
- *"There is no following Jesus apart from the church."* What might that mean?
- What are our responsibilities to one another?

**Say:**

> The Spirit gives us all gifts to use so that the body of Christ can function. God has gifted each member of the church for some form of ministry — of performing sacrificial love. Because the gift is **for us,** it is personal. Because the gift is **for God's world,** it is communal.

## Rewrite the Metaphor

Have the youth rewrite **1 Corinthians 12:12–27** using a **new metaphor.** Invite them to choose one: a tree, a machine, a symphony orchestra, a sports team. For example:

> *"For just as the orchestra is one and has many musicians, and all the musicians of the orchestra, though many, are one orchestra, so it is with Christ."*

Share a few out loud.

## Planning Children & Youth Sunday

Go over the upcoming Children & Youth Sunday service. These are the ways this year's class can share their gifts:

- **Welcome / announcements** — 2 students
- **Sermon** — one 10-minute or two 5-minute messages
  - Possible topics: journey of faith; Deuteronomy 26 (*"a wandering Aramean was my father"*) with a recitation of the history of God's people; the Road to Emmaus
- **Scripture reader** — student
- **Tech team** — video, slides, sound (3 students)
- **Greeters** — 3 students (youth / children)

Reference materials on the **[Children & Youth Sunday](../../events/children-and-youth-sunday/)** page: welcome template, student sermon, student scriptures, and past program samples.
