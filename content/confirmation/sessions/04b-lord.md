**Part B of Session 4 · Lord.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules; **collect phones**
- Whiteboard + dry-erase markers
- Note cards, pens
- Print and cut out the four scenarios from the appendix
- Electronic way to show a picture (smartphone / tablet / laptop)
- Optional: *Books of the New Testament* infographic

Write **"Who is your Lord?"** on the whiteboard.

## Game — Jesus as Lord Is _______

Go around the room. Each person finishes the sentence: *Jesus as Lord is ______.* Whoever is the last one standing wins a prize.

*Sample fills:* Jesus as Lord is full of grace and truth, holy, just, working with God, forgiving, compassionate, powerful, healing, loving, wise, a servant, has authority, desires to give us rest, wants to give us abundant life — and immeasurably more.

**The winner gets the opportunity to pray for the class.**

## Read — Philippians 2:1–13

**Big question (recap):** *Who is your Lord?* This lesson is about how the answer impacts how we live.

**Introduce the reading:**

> *(Optional: post the* Books of the New Testament *infographic and ask a volunteer to find "Philippians" and note who wrote it.)*
>
> The letter to the church in Philippi was written by the Apostle Paul about **20 to 30 years after Jesus lived,** and it contains an early hymn to Jesus as Lord.
>
> In Rome, the emperors were considered to be gods. Romans required all the peoples they conquered not only to honor their emperor but also to **worship** him as a god and call him **"Lord."** That was a line neither Jews nor Christians were willing to cross. They would not call Caesar "Lord," and they would not bow down to worship him.

**Show the picture** at <https://bit.ly/2jTWojp>. These ruins are what is left of a fountain in the city of Ephesus. The fountain was built in the second century to honor Roman Emperor Trajan. It included a huge statue of Trajan. These words were inscribed on it: *"I am the ruler of the world. The world is under my foot."* Ironically, today only Trajan's foot and a globe remain.

*(If you have the photo of your leader visiting Ephesus on hand, show it here for a laugh.)*

**Say:**

> There is only one ruler of the world.

Open Bibles and get **three volunteers** to read **Philippians 2:1–13** — verses 1–4, 5–11, 12–13.

> ***Philippians 2:5–11 — In your relationships with one another, have the same mindset as Christ Jesus: who, being in very nature God, did not consider equality with God something to be used to his own advantage; rather, he made himself nothing by taking the very nature of a servant, being made in human likeness. And being found in appearance as a man, he humbled himself by becoming obedient to death — even death on a cross!***
>
> ***Therefore God exalted him to the highest place and gave him the name that is above every name, that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, in heaven and on earth and under the earth, and every tongue acknowledge that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.***

**Ask:**

1. Given that the Romans demanded that all people call the emperor "Lord," what kind of fallout would come to Christians for calling *Jesus* "Lord"?
   - *(Persecuted. Laughed at. Not included in social circles. People thinking they were weird. Not getting jobs or opportunities.)*
2. If they were going to face these consequences, **why** would they call Jesus their Lord?
   - *(Because he brings them abundant life.)*
3. If Jesus is Lord, how is he *different* from so many rulers of the world?
   - *(Hint: v.5–11. He doesn't use his power for his own gain but for the gain of others.)*
4. Should honoring Jesus as Lord — the highest authority of our lives — make a difference in how we act or what we say?

## Scenarios

Break students into four groups of 3–4. Each group gets one scenario. Have them come up with **two responses** — one if Jesus is Lord, one if Jesus is not — and act them out after a few minutes.

Scenarios:

1. **Someone is talking badly about someone else in the locker room.**
2. **The person next to you in class offers to give you the answers to the quiz.**
3. **There are dishes in the sink when you get home from school.**
4. **You are invited to go to the soup kitchen to feed people who are homeless Saturday morning.**

## The Mind of Christ

Ask for a volunteer who can draw. On the whiteboard, draw a large outline of a human head — large enough to fill the board.

Call on the youth to draw inside the head any **symbols, words, or images that signify having the same mind as Christ.** These could be Jesus' attitudes, actions, or characteristics — things Paul calls Christians to imitate.

**Ask:** Who is someone you know that has the mindset of Christ? Why?

Pass out note cards and pens. Have students consider which particular qualities they would like to practice in their own lives and write them down.

**Ask:**

- Would anyone share some of the qualities you wrote down?
- Is it easy to live up to these qualities?
  - **Accountability is helping intentions become reality.** What are some ways we can hold each other accountable?
- What are some ways we can celebrate when we see one another having the mind of Christ?

## The Big Question

**Say:**

> The big question this unit is *"Who is your Lord?"* You don't have to answer out loud — but sit with these two questions:

- Are you ready to claim Jesus Christ as your Lord? Why or why not?
- Are you ready to live in a way that shows loyalty to your Lord? Why or why not?

## Optional — Rap It Out

If there's time: make a rap song about Jesus being Lord. Have partners choose one characteristic of Christ and make two rhyming lines. Go around and say the rap.
