**Part A of Session 9 · Fellowship.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules; choose someone to pray
- Paper and pens for every student
- Bibles

## Opener — Koinonia

**Ask:** Can anyone guess what this Greek word means?

> **Koinonia** — *Fellowship.*

**Big question:** *Will you devote yourself to the church's fellowship?*

**Say:**

> **Koinonia** expresses the idea of **sharing in a common bond.** Action is always included in its meaning — it implies *contribution,* *sharing,* and *participation.* Fellowship is not just being together; it's **doing together.**
>
> Doing life together. Repeat after me: **Doing life together.**

## The Priesthood of All Believers

**Say:**

> We want to begin by talking about what the Presbyterian church calls the **priesthood of all believers.**

**Ask:** Any guesses as to what this means?

**Read — 1 Peter 2:4–5:**

> ***Come to him, a living stone, though rejected by mortals yet chosen and precious in God's sight, and like living stones, let yourselves be built into a spiritual house, to be a holy priesthood, to offer spiritual sacrifices acceptable to God through Jesus Christ.***

**Say:**

> The priesthood of all believers asserts that **all humans have access to God through Christ,** the true high priest, and thus do not need a priestly mediator.
>
> Priests acted as the middle person between us and God. Priests of this nature are still used in the Catholic and Orthodox traditions, but when the notion of the priesthood of all believers was introduced in the Reformation, it made the claim that **all Christians were equal.**
>
> The priesthood of all believers also signifies that **all members of the body of Christ can speak and contribute** to the life of the church.
>
> So, as we are called to fellowship — *koinonia,* doing life together — it's a fellowship *as the priesthood of all believers,* where we all have equal access to God.

## The Two Marks of Koinonia: Gathering and Love

**Say:**

> The fellowship — *koinonia* — of the church is ultimately marked by two things: **gathering** and **love.**

### First — Gathering

*Ask a volunteer to read* **Hebrews 10:24–25:**

> ***And let us consider how we may spur one another on toward love and good deeds, not giving up meeting together, as some are in the habit of doing, but encouraging one another — and all the more as you see the Day approaching.***

**Answer in your group, or as a family:**

1. What is one of your favorite memories gathering at a church function?
2. In what ways can your family gather with the people of God? *(Church on Sunday, dinner / prayer with other Christian families, events like Pancake Dinner, mission trips, etc.)*
3. Why might you need to **encourage one another** when it comes to continuing to gather?
4. What are some spaces you want to commit to gathering in *after* this confirmation class is over? *(Church, Sunday School, youth group, retreats, etc.)*

### Second — Love

**Introduce the reading:**

> This story takes place while Jesus and his disciples are eating the Passover dinner — what people sometimes call the **Last Supper.** In Jesus' day, most people traveled on foot wearing sandals. Their feet quickly got very dusty. In well-to-do households, a servant would wash a visitor's feet when they entered. This job was considered **the lowliest of all the servant tasks.** In this encounter, Jesus offers the disciples a vivid example of how they are to be a loving community based on his example.

*Read* **John 13:3–5, 14–15:**

> ***Jesus, knowing that the Father had given all things into his hands, and that he had come from God and was going to God, got up from the table, took off his outer robe, and tied a towel around himself. Then he poured water into a basin and began to wash the disciples' feet and to wipe them with the towel that was tied around him... "So if I, your Lord and Teacher, have washed your feet, you also ought to wash one another's feet. For I have set you an example, that you also should do as I have done to you."***

**Ask** *(in small groups if you have time; otherwise big group):*

- What would be like foot-washing today? *(Washing the dishes? Scrubbing the bathroom? Cleaning up someone else's mess at a church supper? Spending time with a lonely or demanding person at lunch?)*

**Say:**

> Refer back to **agape** — *sacrificial love.* It takes action.

**Ask:**

- When do you ever see followers of Jesus washing others' feet?

**Read:**

> ***John 13:35 — "By this everyone will know that you are my disciples, if you have love for one another."***

**Say:** *Jesus washed even Judas's feet.*

## Naming Gifts

**Say:**

> One of the ways we love in this way is by **sharing our gifts** with one another. Scripture tells us that we've all been given gifts by God to join God at work in the world — loving the world.
>
> As we close, we want to allow others to identify gifts they might see in us.

Ask each confirmand to write their name at the top of a page, then pass the page to the person on the right. Notice the name of the person at the top of the page — *it's okay if you don't know them that well.* Have everyone write (legibly) a gift or talent they think that person has.

*(Share a list of sample gifts / talents to prime the pump.)*

Pass the papers around the circle. When everyone's page is full, have each student read their own page aloud. Prompt this response:

> **Leader:** *"Thank you, God, for NAME who brings us the gift of GIFT."*
>
> **Group:** *"Thank you, God, for good gifts."*
