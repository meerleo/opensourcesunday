**Part A of Session 8 · Teaching.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules
- Note cards + pens
- Printed **Resource 8a** (Common English Bible + *The Message* paraphrases of 2 Timothy 1:9–10) — enough for everyone or partners
- Bibles
- The BIG Journal (or equivalent) if your program uses one; page references below assume it

Write the big question on the board:

> **WILL YOU DEVOTE YOURSELF TO THE CHURCH'S TEACHING?**

## Opener

Confirmation ground rules + highs and lows go-around.

**Say:**

> Today we're going to look at the **Apostles' Creed** — a statement of faith that is over a thousand years old, unites multiple denominations of Christianity, and is based in what was professed by those in the early church.

## I Believe / I Love

*(Follow page 48 in the Teacher's Guide.)*

Direct each person (or pair) to write the words **"I believe"** at the top of their note card. Have them write **four things:**

1. One thing they believe **about God**
2. One thing they believe **about Jesus**
3. One thing they believe **about the Holy Spirit**
4. One thing they believe **about the church**

Now flip the card over (or use a fresh one) and write **"I love"** at the top. Do the same thing with love instead of belief.

**Ask:**

- Which was the hardest belief for you to write? *(Most groups say the Holy Spirit.)*
- Was it easier to name what you *believe* about God, or what you *love* about God? Why?
- Is there a connection between what you believe about someone and what you love about someone? Explain.

**Pray:**

> *Thank you for all the faithful people who have come before us. Help us to learn more about you. Where we have questions, give us patience and a willingness to listen for answers. Where we are confident, give us patience and a willingness to share our faith in new ways. Give us the willingness to listen to others and to speak honestly. Amen.*

## Read — 2 Timothy 1

**Say:**

> First and Second Timothy and Titus are called the **Pastoral Letters** (or Pastoral Epistles) in the New Testament. The Apostle Paul — or another church leader writing in Paul's name — sent these letters to pastors with advice about how to face the challenges of standing firm in the faith.

Distribute Bibles. Invite volunteers to read **Acts 16:1–2** and **1 Thessalonians 3:2, 5–6** to learn a bit about Timothy.

Have participants turn to **2 Timothy 1.** Ask a volunteer to read aloud verses 1–14.

**Ask:**

- What is the problem Paul is addressing in the letter?
- Why do you think Paul mentions Timothy's mother and grandmother? (v.5)
- What is the *sound teaching* Paul is referring to? Why is it important that Christians *"hold to the standard of sound teaching"* (v.13)?
- Which of Paul's words to Timothy do you find most encouraging? Why?

To help participants understand v.6, ask a volunteer to find and read aloud **Acts 13:2–3.** Point out that the Spirit was given to Paul through the laying on of hands, and Paul gave this gift to Timothy.

Have participants read aloud **2 Timothy 1:9–10** in unison. Tell them scholars think these verses quote an **early Christian creed** — words of faith that early Christians recited in worship.

**Ask:** What word or phrase from these verses grabs your attention the most? Why?

## Paraphrase Exercise

Distribute copies of **Resource 8a** (CEB + *The Message*). Ask volunteers to read the verses aloud.

Ask each participant to write a paraphrase of **2 Timothy 1:9–10** (BIG Journal p.74) as either an *"I believe"* or *"I love"* statement. Ask volunteers to read their paraphrases to the group.

**Ask:**

- What important things do we learn about what Christians believe from these verses?
- How might speaking creeds together in worship encourage Christians?

## The Apostles' Creed

**Say:**

> A **creed** helps us describe what we affirm as Christians. The word *creed* has the connotation of *"I give my heart"* and is often translated as *"I believe."*
>
> The Apostles' Creed was developed over a few hundred years after Jesus lived. It's based on Scripture and the understanding and experience of the earliest Christians.

**Parallel example:**

> Centuries after the Constitution was written, Martin Luther King Jr. argued for abolishing segregation based on the Declaration of Independence (*all men are created equal*). He appealed to a document everyone held to be authoritative in how this country should run. The Apostles' Creed is our version of that — a document the church has agreed names what it stands on.

Read the **Apostles' Creed** aloud (BIG Journal p.72–73). A printable copy: <https://www.pcabookstore.com/samples/11589.pdf>

**Ask:**

- Why is it important to notice that the creed begins with the words *"I believe in..."*?

## Close

**Say:**

> We'll revisit the Apostles' Creed and learn more about our own worship service next week.

**Close in prayer.**
