**Part A of Session 6 · Disciple.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules
- Bibles for all
- **Things to make a cross** with — sticks, twine, popsicle sticks, whatever's on hand
- A downstairs / outdoor space to move to for the cross-making activity

**Big question for the next two weeks:** *What does it mean to be a disciple of Jesus?*

## Opening

**Ask:** Would you say that being a Christian is **safe or risky?** Why?

*(Teacher: give a personal story about a time your faith cost you something.)*

**Ask:** Sometimes we confuse *belief* and *discipleship.* Does anyone know the difference?

**Say:**

> **Belief** implies something inward. **Discipleship** implies something active — a change in one's daily life.

**Ask:** What is the central symbol of Christianity?

> *(The cross.)*

**Say:**

> Following Jesus is not an easy thing. It means taking up the cross.

## Read — Mark 8:31–38

**Introduce the reading:**

> In Jesus' time, people expected God to send a Messiah — *Christ,* "anointed one." Most people assumed the Messiah would lead the people of Israel in a nationalistic revolution against Rome. Peter thought Jesus would be *that* kind of Messiah. So when Jesus says that he must suffer, be rejected and killed, and after three days rise again — Peter reacts.

*Ask a volunteer to read aloud* **Mark 8:31–33.**

**Ask:**

- Why do you think Peter rebukes (reprimands, criticizes) Jesus?
- Why does Jesus tell Peter to *get behind him?*
  - *Disciples follow Jesus and his teachings — not the other way around.*

*Ask another volunteer to read aloud* **Mark 8:34–38.**

**Ask:**

- What do you think Jesus means by *"take up their cross"* (v.34)?
  - *Disciples should be prepared to face sacrifice and challenges like Jesus' own life and death — when their beliefs and values conflict with those of worldly authorities.*
- How do you think Jesus' disciples reacted to his call to *take up their cross?*
- What does it look like to *lose one's life for Jesus' sake and for the sake of the gospel* (v.35)?

## Cross Activity

**Move downstairs (or outside).** Make crosses together. As you work, consider Jesus' call to take up your own cross — to lay down your life for the sake of others.

**Ask:**

- What were you thinking of as you constructed your cross?
- How did you feel — how do you feel — about the call to discipleship?
