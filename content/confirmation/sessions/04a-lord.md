## Big Group — What Does "Lord" Mean?

Recap the table answers.

**Ask:** What does *Lord* mean?

> *Someone or something having power, authority, or influence; a master or ruler.*

**Big question for the next two weeks:** *Who is your Lord?*

The next two lessons are about where our ultimate loyalties lie — and how that impacts the way we live.

## Read — Exodus 3:13–15 (The Burning Bush)

*Have people take turns reading with emotional inflections: narrator, Moses, God.*

**Introduce the reading:**

> Moses has fled Egypt and is living in Midian. He's married, looking after the flocks of his father-in-law, Jethro. In search of fresh pasture, Moses arrives at Mount Horeb — *"the mountain of God."* In this holy place, God speaks to him through a burning bush that will not be consumed by fire. In this encounter, **God's name is revealed to Moses.**

> ***Exodus 3:13–15 — Moses said to God, "Suppose I go to the Israelites and say to them, 'The God of your fathers has sent me to you,' and they ask me, 'What is his name?' Then what shall I tell them?"***
>
> ***God said to Moses, "I am who I am. This is what you are to say to the Israelites: 'I am has sent me to you.'"***
>
> ***God also said to Moses, "Say to the Israelites, 'The Lord, the God of your fathers — the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac and the God of Jacob — has sent me to you.' This is my name forever, the name you shall call me from generation to generation."***

**Ask:**

- What is God's name according to v.14? *(In Hebrew this is* Yahweh*.)*
- What do you think this name means?

**Say:**

> "I AM" is the ultimate statement of **self-sufficiency** and **being ever-present.** God is able to do what he wills to do and accomplish what he wills to accomplish. He is utterly dependable, present, and powerful. In other words: **God is the Lord of all.**

## Competition — Jesus as Lord Is ______

**Say:**

> We know God is the all-powerful Lord. But just what *kind* of Lord is he? Well — Jesus shows us.

**Split into groups and divvy up the Bible passages from the appendix.** Each group reads their passages together.

**Say:**

> This is a competition. We're going to go from table to table asking you to fill in this statement: *"Jesus as Lord is ______."* You have about five minutes to look at the scriptures and come up with as many answers as you can. **Write them down.**
>
> After five minutes, go in turn to each table — **no repeats.** Winner is the last one standing.

**Read together to close:**

> ***John 10:10 — "I have come that they may have life, and have it to the full."***

**Say:** This is the kind of Lord that Jesus is.

## The Other Lords

**Say:**

> The reality is: it's **not** *if* we'll have a Lord. It's *who* or *what* will be our Lord. There are many things that try to have authority, power, and influence over us — that try to claim the ultimate lordship over our lives. Some aren't necessarily bad; some are more sinister. But none of them deserve the number-one spot.
>
> The Scriptures name and warn us about these things often. Here's an example from the Apostle Paul's letter to the church in Philippi:

> ***Philippians 3:19 — "Their end is destruction, their god is the belly, and their glory is in their shame; their minds are set on earthly things."***

**Ask at tables:** What other things might try to have lordship over our lives? *(Remember: **Lord** = someone or something having power, authority, or influence; a master or ruler.)*

*(Possible answers: grades, work, productivity, power, money, possessions, popularity, social media, video games, sports, relationships, friends, food.)*

**Say:**

> Compare these with the descriptions you came up with for **Jesus as Lord.** Notice the contrast — but don't knock those things entirely.
>
> Jesus as Lord is **full of grace and truth, holy, seeks to do justice, cares for the poor, is forgiving, compassionate, powerful, healing, loving, wise, humble, has all authority, desires to give us rest,** and immeasurably more.
>
> Those other things aren't necessarily all bad. But only when we give Jesus the top spot do we experience the fullest life there is.

> ***John 10:10 — "I have come that they may have life, and have it to the full."***

**Say:**

> Being a follower of Jesus means declaring the bold statement that **Jesus is Lord of our lives.** Next week we'll discuss what this means for how we live.

## Family Challenge

Before we pray out, a family challenge:

> Choose **one thing** you'll do over the next week to declare that Jesus is Lord of your family. Examples: pray before bed or a meal; set aside the phone or TV for an hour to pray and spend quality time with family; go to church together; read the Bible together (*maybe Exodus 3?!*); sing a worship song together; read a Psalm together in the morning.

If your family is here, work with them. If not, think about it — and take it home to them.

## Prayer — Turning Out

Get everyone in a circle. Cross your right arm over your left and take the hand of the person on either side.

Ask each person to **say a word that describes Jesus Christ.**

Then pray:

> *Lord Jesus, you are all of these and more. Help us to follow your ways more closely as we leave this place. Amen.*

Ask participants to keep their hands joined as they raise their right arms and move *under* them — creating a circle of joined hands with everyone now facing **outward.**

**Say to the group:**

> *When we turn to Jesus, we turn to the world he loves. Share the good news.*

## Alternate Track — Elijah on Mount Carmel

Source material includes an alternate / extended passage for the *which Lord will you follow?* question: **1 Kings 18:20–39** (Elijah vs. the prophets of Baal).

**Introduce the reading:**

> King Ahab was the worst king of Israel. He and his wife, Jezebel, imposed the worship of Baal (Baal means "lord") on the people. Baal was a Canaanite nature god. The question for the people of God: which lord would they serve — Baal or YHWH? The story continues as King Ahab brought 450 prophets of Baal against Elijah, the one prophet of YHWH. It was an epic showdown. This wasn't some typical disagreement over religion — King Ahab was actually killing off the prophets of God, essentially forcing people to worship Baal.

*Have people take turns reading* **1 Kings 18:20–39** *with emotional inflections: Elijah, prophets of Baal, the crowd, the narrator.*

**Ask:**

- Listen for what you think is the most important word / phrase / image / object / event in the passage. We'll explain our choices afterward.
- Why was it so important for the people to decide which Lord to follow?
  - Look back at the first commandment — ***Exodus 20:2–3 — "I am the Lord your God, who brought you out of Egypt, out of the land of slavery. You shall have no other gods before me."***
  - **Our lives are defined by our Lord.**
