**Part B of Session 7 · Love.**

{{< callout type="warning" >}}
**Get right to it** — the campus tour takes longer than you think.
{{< /callout >}}

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules; invite someone to pray
- Let the mission-room / food-pantry leaders know you're coming
- Optional: coordinate an activity at the food pantry (making food bags, etc.) if your church has one
- Invite a church member with a compelling service-and-faith story to speak for 5–10 minutes

**Big question:** *Will you show Christ's love?*

## Recap

**Ask:** What does it mean to show Christ's love?

**Ask:** What two types of love showed up in last week's passage?

> *Phileo and agape.*

**Ask:** What's the difference between the two?

Recap **John 21:15–19.**

**Ask:** When are you a sheep? When are you a feeder?

## Walking Tour — How the Church Shows Christ's Love

**Ask:** What are the different ways *this church* shows Christ's love?

Then take a field trip through the building. Walk the whole campus. At each stop, name what happens there and how it's an expression of Christ's love. A template tour (adapt to your church):

- **Prayer pit / chapel** — *it all starts with prayer*
- **Mission room** — mission trips, water walks, partnerships; walk through the things on the walls
- **Music / arts room** — music, gifts
- **Kitchen** — meals, delivering meals to shut-ins
- **Fellowship hall** — giving, youth group
- **Band closet / supply room** — food bags for homeless-service partners, band
- **"Man cave" / utility room** — mission shoes for Guatemala, middle-school missions, senior care, buildings and grounds
- **Preschool / nursery** — Sunday school, preschool
- **Food Pantry** — service days (Monday & Wednesday, in our case)
- **Pastoral care / connections office** — getting people connected, the good-neighbor fund, volunteer coordination
- **Conference room** — committees (admin, HR), prayer meetings, elders, deacons
- **Sanctuary** — music, preaching, tech, Ash Wednesday, communion, tithing, weddings, funerals
- **Graveyard** — care for those who have passed away

**Bonus:** If Ash Wednesday is coming up, ask who can help with **set up** and who can read the **confession.**

## Activity — Food Pantry / Food Bags

Head to the food pantry and put together food bags.

Invite a leader to share briefly:

- How did you get involved?
- What do you do there?
- How has it impacted your faith and the way you show Christ's love?
- How could *we* get involved?

## Discussion — Faithful Disciple, Showing His Love

> ***"Will you be Christ's faithful disciple, ... showing his love?"***

**Ask:**

- How does *doing* the work of love help you understand who Jesus Christ is for you?
- Why do you think **sacrificial love (agape)** is important for following Jesus and showing his love?
- Why do you think **mutual affection (phileo)** is important for following Jesus and showing his love?
- How will you show **agape** and **phileo** to others as a part of the church?

## Close

Have students come up with **their own ways to love** this week — one specific thing, one specific person. Bless them in prayer.
