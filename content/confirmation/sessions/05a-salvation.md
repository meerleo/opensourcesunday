**Part A of Session 5 · Salvation.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules; ask who will open in prayer next week
- Whiteboard + markers
- Phones or laptops for the Blooket game
- A guest lined up to share their salvation testimony (about 10 minutes)

## Icebreaker

**Ask:**

- What is your favorite thing about Thanksgiving?
- Do you have any family Thanksgiving traditions?
- What is your *least* favorite thing about Thanksgiving?

## The Big Question

**Write on the board:** *"Who is your Savior?"*

These next two weeks, we're talking about this question.

## What Is a Savior?

Have students **google** the word *Savior.*

You're likely to see two definitions:

1. *A person who saves someone or something (especially a country or cause) from danger, and who is regarded with the veneration of a religious figure.*
   - Synonyms: rescuer, liberator, deliverer, emancipator, champion, knight in shining armor, friend in need, good Samaritan, salvation.
2. *(In Christianity) God or Jesus Christ as the redeemer of sin and saver of souls.*
   - Synonyms: Christ, Jesus, Jesus Christ, Redeemer, Messiah, Lord.

**Ask:** So — what do we need to be *saved from?*

## Game — Blooket

Play a Blooket set on *"things that claim to save us."* Ours is here (use the code when prompted):

- <https://dashboard.blooket.com/set/655bb11da92a368a39ff7f5a>

**Ask:**

- Look at the advertisements, products, and institutions on the board. Why do these places look for *problems* people have and make *promises* to fix them?

## The Presbyterian Promise — GRACE

**Ask:** In the Presbyterian religion, what is *our* promise?

**Say:**

> Our promise is **GRACE.** Grace fully known in the love and sacrifice of Jesus.

Write this on the board:

> **G** od's
> **R** iches
> **A** t
> **C** hrist's
> **E** xpense

**Say:**

> Presbyterians believe that God promises a solution to our brokenness and the brokenness of the world in the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ. We call that **salvation.**
>
> Salvation is God's gift — to save us from all that is against God, and to give us abundant life here and now, *and* in eternity.

## Testimony

Introduce the guest who will share their salvation story. After they finish, open it up for questions.

## Close

**Close with a popcorn prayer** — each person prays one short sentence or word out loud, popping up as they feel led.
