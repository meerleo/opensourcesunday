## Read — Covenant

**Say:**

> The reason we're talking about promises is because the Confirmation class journey is all about **covenant.**
>
> We first see the term *covenant* in the Old Testament. Covenant is a legal term that binds two parties together. In the Bible, we see that God *initiates* the covenant with us. It's a promise with the intent of relationship.
>
> We see this covenant-making between God and humanity all over Scripture, but look with me to Deuteronomy 7:9.

> ***Deuteronomy 7:9 — "Know therefore that the Lord your God is God; he is the faithful God, keeping his covenant of love to a thousand generations of those who love him and keep his commandments."***

**Say:**

> There are two parties in this divine covenant: **God and us.**
>
> Scripture describes God's covenant with us — and we know God meant what he said because: **Jesus.**
>
> Jesus coming and walking among us, teaching us how to live and to love, and then giving his life to pay the cost of our sin, freeing us from being separated from God — **Jesus is the fulfillment of God's covenant to us.** God makes his covenant *and* fulfills his end of it, because he loves us.
>
> We're going to spend this Confirmation journey getting to know God through Jesus.
>
> **Who is God? Who does God say we are? And how has God called each of you to partner with him in the world?**
>
> Confirmation is a time for asking questions — so ask all of the questions you want.

## The Three-Way Confirmation

**Say:**

> So that's God's part — but **what about us?** Relationships take two. Like the text we read, it goes both ways. While God is the initiator and the ultimate fulfiller of the covenant, he invites us to respond by following him and loving his people.

**Ask:**

- Who here has been baptized? Who hasn't? *(If you haven't, you'll have the opportunity.)*
- Does anyone know why we get baptized?

**Say:**

- Individuals are received into the church to share in its life and ministry. It's a statement of your identity as a beloved child of God — of God choosing you.
- At baptism, the parents, the sponsors, and the congregation publicly **promise** to help raise these (typically) infant children into adults.
  - Those adults include Sunday School teachers, mentors at the youth ministry, and every adult in the congregation.

**Say:**

> So other than being an example of a covenantal promise, why am I talking about baptism?
>
> Because going through Confirmation is really a **three-way confirmation:**

1. **The Holy Spirit** confirms our baptismal identity as a child of God.
2. **The church** confirms your gifts and membership in the body of Christ.
3. **You** confirm your faith in Jesus and the teachings of the church.
   - At the end of this process, this is **your** decision.

## The Big Four (Preview)

**Say:**

> You will be asked what we call the **big four questions** — questions we'll spend the entire year talking about and looking to see what the Bible has to say about them.
>
> So:
>
> - Confirmation isn't a graduation. It might be a new stage in your faith. It might be the start of making your faith your own. But it isn't meant to end here.
> - We want to learn, but we also want to have fun. Life with God is an adventure.
>
> Because faith is a journey we all take together — and like any journey, it requires commitment.

## Commitments of Confirmation Class

### Students

- **Attendance each week.** (You can miss if you're sick, if there's a special event, or a family gathering — but make this a priority.)
- **Be in church.**
- **Engage with your parents at home.**
- **Be honest.**
- **No phones.**

### Parents

- **Thank you for driving your kids.** It is exhausting to drive to practice, drive to youth group, drive to Sunday School, drive them to friends' houses. Thank you, thank you, thank you.
- **Lead your son or daughter by example.** Attend worship. Pray at meals. Model what it means to follow Jesus. Be honest about your faith, including the struggles and the doubts.
  - And attend the few parent classes on the calendar.
- **Communicate with us regularly** and spend some time each week engaging in your student's Confirmation journey. We know you have a lot going on, so we want to make that as easy as possible.

### Our Commitment to You

- We're committed to walking with you and your family in faith.
  - The teachers will divide up the class and check in with you regularly.
  - We're committed to wrestling alongside students as they explore their faith and ask questions.
- We'll send an email every other week recapping what we learned and giving you a practice for at home.
