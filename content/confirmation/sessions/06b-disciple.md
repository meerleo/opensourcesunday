**Part B of Session 6 · Disciple.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules
- Church bulletins (one per student)
- Note cards, pens
- Whiteboard + markers

**Big question continues:** *What does it mean to be a disciple of Jesus?*

## Recap

**Ask:** Who remembers what it means to be a disciple?

> *To take up your cross and follow Jesus. To make sacrifices for the sake of God and others.*

## Read — Acts 7:51–60 (Stephen, the First Martyr)

**Say:**

> Acts is a history of the early church, written by Luke. Stephen is the **first martyr** — he stood up for his faith regardless of the consequences.

*Ask a volunteer to read aloud* **Acts 7:51–60.**

**Ask:**

- Are there ways in which you stand up for your faith at home or at school?
- What is difficult about standing up for your faith?
- What does this story tell us about discipleship?

**Say:**

> Following Jesus is rewarding — but it is also risky. Christ doesn't call us to play it safe. He calls us to sacrifice and to stand up for our faith.

**Ask:**

- How do you think Christian discipleship (denying self and taking up the cross) compares to loving God, neighbors, and self with mind, soul, and heart — in other words, with everything you have? How are they different?

## Notecards — Courage to Stand Firm

Pass out note cards and pens. Ask confirmands to write down **two situations** in which it would take courage to stand firm in their faith and stay true to Jesus' teachings.

**Write these possibilities on the board to get them thinking:**

- Seeing someone being bullied
- Being with friends who are gossiping about others
- Being around someone who says people who believe in God are dumb
- Choosing to honor commitments at church when others are not
- Observing family members wasting resources or doing something harmful
- Seeing a friend who is a Christian being hateful toward someone else

**Ask:**

- What do you risk sacrificing by showing courage and standing firm?
- What good things might happen if you do?

## Read — Mark 12:41–44 (The Generous Widow)

*Ask a volunteer to read aloud.*

**Ask:**

- Why was the woman's gift worth more than all those who put in a lot more money than she did?
- What does this story tell us about discipleship?

## Read — Matthew 20:20–28 (The Greatest in the Kingdom)

*Ask a volunteer to read aloud.*

**Ask:**

- What kind of kingdom do you think the mother of John and James thought Jesus was going to have?
- Why do you think the others were angry?
- What does this story tell us about discipleship?

## Worship as the Gymnasium of Discipleship

Hand out copies of your church's worship service bulletin. Write the components on the board.

**Say:**

> Sunday worship is like a gymnasium — it's meant to *train* us to be disciples of Christ throughout the week.

**Ask:**

- What is your favorite part?
- Which parts of the service require *responses* from us?
  - Where does the worship service call on church members to deny themselves? To give sacrificially?
- What, if anything, would you change in our worship so that *sacrificially following Jesus* was emphasized more?

## Looking Ahead — Your Own Confirmation

**Say:**

> We follow Jesus *together.* Being a disciple of Jesus is about training together in the sacrificial love of Jesus. As we look ahead to confirmation:

**Ask:**

- What sacrifices do you think you might be asked to make because you are being confirmed?

## Commitment — I Will Follow Jesus

Write the following phrase on the board:

> *"I will follow Jesus. Even though following him might put me at risk, I want to stand up for ______________."*

Look back at your notecard and choose a situation (or make a new one) where you want to stand for the right thing.

One at a time, invite students to **stand and say:** *"I will follow Jesus. Even though following him might put me at risk, I want to stand up for ____________"* — naming a person, cause, or ministry.

Prompt the group's response: *"I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me."* (Philippians 4:13)

*(Teacher: be prepared to stand and speak first to prompt others.)*

When everyone is standing, join hands as the church and pray together for the strength to follow Jesus in your daily lives.

Tell confirmands you will pray for them this week and invite them to pray for you.
