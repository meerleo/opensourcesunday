**Part B of Session 11 · Prayer.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules
- Four sets of **situation / scripture strips** (see appendix) cut apart into two piles
- Optional: prayer-walk materials (for a park or sanctuary walk)
- Optional: printed **finger labyrinths** (one per student)

## Opener — Matching Prayer with Scripture

Break the group into 3–4 smaller groups. Give each group a set of **situation strips** and a set of **scripture strips.** Have them **match the scriptures to the situations.** *(Appendix at the bottom of this page.)*

Invite them to come back and go through them together.

**Say:**

> When we don't know what to pray, we can pray the words of Scripture.
>
> We can also pray the **Lord's Prayer.**

## The Lord's Prayer

**Pray together:**

> *Our Father who art in heaven,*
> *hallowed be thy name.*
> *Thy kingdom come, thy will be done,*
> *on earth as it is in heaven.*
> *Give us this day our daily bread,*
> *and forgive us our debts,*
> *as we forgive our debtors;*
> *and lead us not into temptation,*
> *but deliver us from evil.*
> *For thine is the kingdom, and the power, and the glory, forever. Amen.*

**Ask:**

- What challenges you about prayer?
- Do we have to say the *right words* when we pray?
- Do you think there's anything **off-limits?** Anger? Sadness? Questioning?

**Say:**

> **No.** All prayer is music to God's ears. The main point is that we **commit to praying** and seek to build a relationship with God. Though — like the Lord's Prayer and the words of Scripture (especially the Psalms, the book of prayer in the Bible) — we have been given some awesome models of prayer.

## Optional Deep Dives

Pick one (or more) of the following as time allows.

### Option A — Prayer Walk in the Park

Create a prayer walk in a nearby park incorporating:

- Silence
- Scripture
- Repetition
- The five senses

### Option B — ACTS Prayer

Teach the **ACTS** framework:

- **A**doration
- **C**onfession
- **T**hanksgiving
- **S**upplication

Have students try and fill in prayer phrases for each.

### Option C — Finger Labyrinth

Hand out printed finger labyrinths. Have each student turn around to face away from the group, or spread out.

**Say:**

> For centuries, Christians have walked along the paths of labyrinths to focus their attention on praying and listening to God. Start by relaxing and taking a few deep breaths. As you move your finger along the labyrinth path, say one of the **breath prayers** by breathing in the first phrase and out the second:

- *Gracious God, / help me to love.*
- *Forgiving God, / have mercy on me.*
- *Holy God, / guide my actions.*
- *Lord Jesus, / help me to follow you.*

**Say:**

> By practicing this type of prayer, you will become less distracted and more comfortable praying this way.

## Discussion

**Ask:**

- What have you learned today that has helped you better understand prayer as having an **open heart** to God?
- What are you still wondering?
- How does praying help you know you are God's beloved child?
- **How will you devote yourself to prayer?**

## Close — The Psalter

Sit in a circle. Have each person read one of the following passages from the book of Psalms. Psalms is the prayer book of the Bible — it has prayers for every occasion and emotion.

- Psalm 9:1
- Psalm 10:17–18
- Psalm 13:5–6
- Psalm 15:2
- Psalm 16:7–8
- Psalm 19:8–9
- Psalm 19:14
- Psalm 26:2–3
- Psalm 27:14
- Psalm 28:7

**Pray:**

> *Gracious God, you know our hearts. We pray that you will enter our hearts, seeping in through our brokenness. Teach us how to pray constantly, so that our lives might be a closer reflection of what you would have us to be in the world. Amen.*

**Close with the Lord's Prayer.**

## Appendix — Situation/Scripture Strips

Print, cut, and distribute to the matching-game groups.

### The situations (praying for one another)

1. In desperation, Sue cheated on a test. Now she feels miserably guilty.
2. At a party, a friend is pressuring you to join in the drinking.
3. The youth group is trying to decide what to do this summer.
4. Within a week of moving to your school, Lee has been labeled a weirdo, a nerd, etc. Others are ignoring Lee. You can see the pain in his face.
5. You got an F on your final exam.
6. Your parents are fighting again.
7. Your best friend is ignoring you to do things with others.
8. You woke up with an enormous pimple on your chin.

### The scriptures

- **Psalm 51:10** — *Create in me a clean heart, O God, and put a new and right spirit within me.*
- **1 Corinthians 3:16** — *Do you not know that you are God's temple and that God's Spirit dwells in you?*
- **1 Peter 2:9** — *But you are a chosen race, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, God's own people, in order that you may proclaim the mighty acts of him who called you out of darkness into his marvelous light.*
- **Matthew 22:37, 39** — *You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, and with all your soul, and with all your mind... You shall love your neighbor as yourself.*
- **Romans 8:38–39** — *For I am convinced that neither death, nor life, nor angels, nor rulers, nor things present, nor things to come, nor powers, nor height, nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God in Christ Jesus our Lord.*
