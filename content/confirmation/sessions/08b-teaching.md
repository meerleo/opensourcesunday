**Part B of Session 8 · Teaching.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules
- Note cards + pens
- **Apostles' Creed matching cards** (kept in the confirmation/retreat room). Enough to pair students; otherwise two-sided copies cut from the teacher's guide. *Collect these in bunches afterward for next year.*
- Smartphones or tablets for students to look up symbols
- Copies of your church's worship bulletin (one per student)
- Whiteboard + markers

Write the big question on the board again:

> **WILL YOU DEVOTE YOURSELF TO THE CHURCH'S TEACHING?**

## Opener

Confirmation ground rules.

**Say:**

> Back to the Apostles' Creed — the summary of our faith that has been the central expression of the church's teaching from the beginning.

Read the creed aloud together.

## Symbols of the Creed

Pass out the **symbol cards.** Have the group research the meaning of the symbols on their phones or tablets.

**Challenge:** draw symbols for the phrases that *aren't* on the cards:

- *"Maker of heaven and earth"*
- *"He descended into hell"*
- *"The quick and the dead"*
- *"The communion of saints"*

Add these to the symbol cards.

*Consider putting them in pairs.* Have them try to put the creed in order **just looking at the symbols.**

## The Cloud of Witnesses

**Say:**

> The phrase *"the communion of saints"* in the Apostles' Creed refers to the relationships among Christians past, present, and future. The New Testament letter called Hebrews describes this communion as a **"cloud of witnesses."** The cloud refers to all the saints who have gone before us as our encouragers in the faith — like our great forebears Abraham and Sarah, or the ones who are great only for *us,* like our parents and teachers in the faith.

*Invite a volunteer to read aloud* **Hebrews 12:1–2.**

**Ask:**

- What images of racing did you notice?
  - *The "cloud of witnesses"* — *the runners who have already finished the race and are cheering on those still competing, identified by name in Hebrews 11.*
  - *"Lay aside every weight"* — *runners of that time ran with little or no clothing, in order to run faster.*
  - *The runner keeping their eyes fixed on the goal, which is Jesus Christ.*
- What words of encouragement did you hear?
- Why might the people who first heard this Scripture have needed words of encouragement?
- Why do *we* need encouragement?

## Worship as Trained Discipleship

Hand out copies of your church's worship service bulletin. Write the main components on the board.

**Say:**

> Sunday worship is like a gymnasium — it's meant to *train and teach* us to be disciples of Christ throughout the week.

**Ask:**

- What is your favorite part?
- Which parts of the service require *responses* from us?
- What, if anything, would you change in our worship so that sacrificially following Jesus was emphasized?

Work through the components of the service and talk about **what they're teaching us** — what they're inviting us into.

**A useful frame:** Isaiah 6. The structure of Christian worship grows out of Isaiah's vision of the throne room.

### Isaiah 6 as the Shape of Worship

> ***Isaiah 6:1 — "In the year that King Uzziah died, I saw the Lord sitting on a throne, high and lofty, and the hem of his robe filled the temple."***

**Worship begins with God.**

> ***v.2–3 — "Seraphs were in attendance above him; each had six wings... And one called to another and said, 'Holy, holy, holy is the Lord of hosts; the whole earth is full of his glory.'"***

**The call to worship** — affirming God's goodness and holiness.

> ***v.4–5 — "The pivots on the thresholds shook at the voices of those who called, and the house filled with smoke. And I said, 'Woe is me! I am lost, for I am a man of unclean lips, and I live among a people of unclean lips, yet my eyes have seen the King, the Lord of hosts!'"***

**Confession** — in response to God's holiness and our sin.

> ***v.6–7 — "Then one of the seraphs flew to me, holding a live coal... The seraph touched my mouth with it and said, 'Now that this has touched your lips, your guilt has departed and your sin is blotted out.'"***

**Assurance of pardon.**

> ***v.8a — "Then I heard the voice of the Lord saying, 'Whom shall I send, and who will go for us?'"***

**Sermon / Scripture** — the word of God.

> ***v.8b — "And I said, 'Here am I; send me!'"***

**Response / offering** — sending.

## Close

Read the **Apostles' Creed** as a closing and pray:

> *Holy God, we praise you for the example of the heroes of faith who have run this race before us. Give us strength and perseverance, and help us fix our eyes on Jesus, in whose name we pray. Amen.*

## Optional — Where Did the Bible Come From?

If you have extra time or the class has questions about scripture, the source material includes an extended teaching on **why we trust the Bible,** including:

- How the canon of the New Testament was formed (through the fourth century)
- Apostolic authority and its transmission through the bishops
- Why the **"telephone game"** argument against Scripture is weak — the care taken by scribes, the volume of manuscripts, the precision vs. other ancient texts
- Why the **"Christianity was invented to keep people in line"** argument is historically inaccurate — Pliny the Younger's 113 AD letter to Trajan describing execution of Christians, and why early Christians had *nothing to gain* from inventing the faith

## Optional — The Barmen Declaration and the Confession of Belhar

If your group has older or more curious confirmands, split into two groups (one teacher each). One goes over the **Barmen Declaration** (Nazi Germany, 1934); the other goes over the **Confession of Belhar** (apartheid South Africa, 1986). The **Confession of 1967** (civil rights, nuclear war) can substitute or supplement.

Hand out copies of **Resource 8f.** Let them use phones to look up the historical background.

**Discuss:**

- What is required of a church to take a stand in times of crisis?
- How can a faith statement that takes a stand create change for good in the world?
- For whom or what would you take a stand in the middle of the cafeteria? In the middle of the congregation?
- Against what evil or injustice in the world would you write a faith statement?
