**Session 12 · Crossing Over.**

*This is the final session of the Confirmation year. Parents are in the room.* Looking back is a small part of tonight; looking **ahead** is the point.

## Before class

- Pens
- Notebooks
- Paper for the river-crossing game (enough "rocks" for each group)
- The **[Statement of Faith](../../statement-of-faith/) packet** — one per confirmand

## Game — River Crossing

Break the group into 3–4 teams. Each team has a stretch of the room to cross and the same number of paper "rocks" as there are people on the team. **Get across the river without touching the water (the floor).**

Rules:

- Can't **throw** or **slide** the paper rocks
- If you touch water, you have to gather all your rocks and start over
- Each team gets 30 seconds to strategize
- Optional: parents vs. kids round

## Transition

**Say:**

> Today we want to talk about **crossing over.** We all have different crossings in our lives — important achievements and transitions from one thing to the next.

**Ask:** What crossings have you experienced?

- **Family life**
- **Personal**
- **School**
- **Church**

*Talk about them at your tables — call on a few to share.*

## Read — Joshua 3:7–17

**Introduce the reading:**

> In the Old Testament we follow the story of the Israelites — God's people. They were enslaved in Egypt for hundreds of years. God worked through Moses to deliver them from Egypt. They were given the Ten Commandments. They built the tabernacle — the temporary mobile space where they worshipped God — and the Ark of the Covenant, where they kept their most sacred possessions. They wandered in the desert for 40 years. Eventually Moses died, and a new leader, Joshua, took his place.
>
> We looked at Joshua at the beginning of the year to talk about the idea of a **covenant** — *a sacred and secure promise for the sake of relationship.*
>
> Tonight our Scripture begins with Joshua and the Israelites standing on the Jordan River. The only thing between them and the Promised Land is the water.

*Ask two volunteers to read:* **Joshua 3:7–13** and **Joshua 3:14–17.**

> ***Joshua 3:14–17 — When the people set out from their tents to cross over the Jordan, the priests bearing the ark of the covenant were in front of the people. Now the Jordan overflows all its banks throughout the time of harvest. So when those who bore the ark had come to the Jordan and the feet of the priests bearing the ark were dipped in the edge of the water, the waters flowing from above stood still, rising up in a single heap far off at Adam, the city that is beside Zarethan, while those flowing toward the sea of the Arabah, the Dead Sea, were wholly cut off. Then the people crossed over opposite Jericho. While all Israel were crossing over on dry ground, the priests who bore the ark of the covenant of the Lord stood firmly on dry ground in the middle of the Jordan, until the entire nation finished crossing over the Jordan.***

**Ask:**

- Who do you think should get the credit for them crossing the river?
  - *(God, Joshua, the people, the priests.)* — *answer in big group*
- Who are the *"priests"* that have helped you along in your Confirmation journey?
  - Think both current and past. — *talk at your table*
- When the Israelites crossed over, was it the end of their journey or the beginning?
  - *(Close of one chapter and start of another.)* — *talk at your table*

**Say:**

> God has brought you to this place. Some leaders have paved the way. You have put in the work. Your parents have walked with you. Your classmates have journeyed with you.
>
> As you prepare to cross over, we want to think together about what lies ahead.

## Looking Ahead — Personal / Communal / Missional

Hand out papers. Have students write their names at the top and work at their tables.

### 1. Personal — get specific

**How will you build your personal relationship with God?**

- Prayer time
- Bible reading
- Devotional book
- Worship music

### 2. Communal

**Where will you commit to gathering with the people of God?**

- Sunday School
- Youth group
- Church
- Christian friends and family

### 3. Missional

**Where will you serve?**

- Mission trips
- Food pantry
- Children's ministry
- Helping lead the youth ministry
- At your school

*Share in the big group.*

## Mentors

On the back of the paper:

- Write down **one or two adults in the church** that you want to stay in touch with *(not your parents).*
- Ask them if they would be a **mentor** to you and continue to walk with you on your faith journey.

## Look Ahead

Go over:

- **Confirmation Dessert** — families welcome; just let us know
- **Confirmation Sunday** — what the service looks like, what's expected of confirmands
- **The Big Four Questions** — what you'll be asked in front of the congregation *(see [Session 1A](../01a-kickoff/#the-big-four))*
- **Calendar** — remaining dates
- **Pictures** — need 3–4 pictures per confirmand spanning baby-to-now
- **Statement of Faith** — hand out the packet and walk through what it is

## Introducing the Statement of Faith

Hand out the packet. See the **[Statement of Faith](../../statement-of-faith/)** section of this site for the full framework, examples, and guides. The high-level points every student should touch on:

- A little about yourself
- Your personal experience with God / how you experience God *(worship, community, serving, art, writing, nature, a Scripture that speaks to you, etc.)*
  - Are there people or places that have impacted your faith?
- What you believe about God *(think Apostles' Creed)*
- How you want to live out your faith
- An affirmation that you want to be a faithful member of the church, and how you want to contribute to its life

The packet includes: sample statements from prior students, the Apostles' Creed, statement-of-faith guides (for generating ideas), and the Big Four Questions. **Students don't have to include nearly everything on those guides** — they're prompts, not checklists.
