**The twelve-topic Confirmation curriculum.**

Twelve topics, most of them split across two class meetings (Part A + Part B) so students have a week between them to sit with the question. The last four topics (**Teaching, Fellowship, Breaking Bread, Prayer**) are the four Acts 2:42 vows a confirmand takes as part of becoming a member. The final session — **Crossing Over** — is the statement-of-faith writing workshop.

## The arc

1. **Covenant** — *what God has always been up to*
2. **Trust** — *knowing and trusting God*
3. **Sin** — *naming what's broken*
4. **Lord** — *Jesus as Lord, not just teacher*
5. **Salvation** — *who is your Savior?*
6. **Disciple** — *following, not just admiring*
7. **Love** — *the shape of Christ's love*
8. **Teaching** — *will you devote yourself to the apostles' teaching?*
9. **Fellowship** — *...and to the fellowship?*
10. **Breaking Bread** — *...and to the breaking of bread?*
11. **Prayer** — *...and to the prayers?*
12. **Crossing Over** — *writing a statement of faith*

{{< callout type="info" >}}
**Pacing.** With A/B pairs, sessions 1–9 and 11 take two meetings each; sessions 10 and 12 take one each. That's **22 meetings** across the academic year — a bi-weekly cadence from September through May with room for a retreat, the Confirmation Dessert, and Confirmation Sunday.
