## Recap

Last week we said that sin is *missing the mark.* Missing the mark at *what?* **Loving God and loving others.**

## Read — Exodus 20:1–17 (The Ten Commandments)

Open Bibles and read **Exodus 20:1–17** together — the Ten Commandments. These are God's specific articulation of what it looks like to love God and love neighbor.

## The Sources of Sin (quick recap)

Little-s sins and big-S Sin, caused by:

- **Satan**
- **Flesh**
- **The World** — the influence of others around us

## Does God Punish Us for Our Sins?

**Say:**

> In the Old Testament, we see that God punishes the people of Israel for their sins — they break the law and receive the consequence. And yet we see that no matter how badly they miss the mark, God always welcomes them back.
>
> In his infinite wisdom and grace, God sends his son Jesus to take on the penalty for our sins once and for all.
>
> Jesus and the New Testament teach us not that we are *necessarily* punished for our sins — but that our sin **separates us from God,** the God who is the only one who provides abundant life.
>
> We are also taught that God does not bless sin, and that in life after death we will be held accountable. **The good news is that the one who will hold us accountable is the same one who gave his life for ours.**
>
> The Bible always takes sin seriously, because it destroys life. Out of a response to that great love, we are called to confront sin and evil in our own lives and in the world.

## Read — Matthew 4:17

> ***Matthew 4:17 — From that time Jesus began to proclaim, "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven has come near."***

**Ask:**

- What does *repent* mean?

**Say:**

> The Greek word is **metanoia** — a *change of mind,* a *turn in direction.* It doesn't mean just feeling bad about what you did. It means actually turning around.

### Discussion

**Ask:**

1. Is it a sin to lie? Why?
2. Is it a sin to not help someone who falls and hurts themselves? Why?
3. Is it a sin if everyone pollutes the lake and the drinking water gets contaminated? Why?
4. Is it a sin if there is a law that prevents people with the name Bob from getting into college? Why?

## Close

**Say:**

> We all miss the mark, and there's a consequence to our sin. **Jesus takes those consequences onto himself and offers us forgiveness** — and sends us out to bring life and repair.
>
> As we spend time with Jesus and his people, it becomes easier and easier to follow him.

**The first of the Big Four vows** — this is what we've been building toward:

> ***Trusting in the gracious mercy of God, do you turn from the way of sin and renounce evil and its power in the world?***
