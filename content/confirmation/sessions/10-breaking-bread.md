**Session 10 · Breaking Bread.**

{{< callout type="info" >}}
**Teach this one over a shared meal, off-campus if possible.** Ours is at a local diner (Woody's). The theology lands differently when the bread on the table is literal.
{{< /callout >}}

**Big question:** *Will you devote yourself to the breaking of bread?*

## Conversation Questions

A short, conversational lesson. Walk the table through each question and invite answers.

**Why do you think we celebrate communion?**

- To come together in unity
- Jesus told us to
- Renewing our covenant with God

### Past Implications

**Ask:** What does communion connect us to in the past?

- We remember Jesus' sacrifice for us and our covenant with God.

### Present Implications

**Ask:** What happens in the present when we take communion?

- We encounter the Holy Spirit through the eating of the bread and the drinking of the juice.

### Future Implications

**Ask:** What does communion point us toward in the future?

- We anticipate the great banquet table of Christ in heaven — where every tear is wiped away and all things are made new.

## The Elements

**Ask:** What does the bread stand for?

- Jesus' body, broken for us in his sacrifice on the cross.

**Ask:** What does the juice / wine stand for?

- Jesus' blood, shed for us for the forgiveness of sins.

## The Table

**Ask:** How big is Jesus' table?

- **Big enough for everyone.**

**Ask:** Why is it important that we break bread *together?*

- We follow Jesus together.
- To remember that we are a unified body.
- To enjoy one another.
- To nurture our souls *and* our bodies.

## Close

If your context allows, consider **sharing communion together** at the table before you leave. Pray for the class and for the church's life to be marked by this meal.
