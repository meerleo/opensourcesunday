**Part A of Session 1 · Covenant.**

The first night. Nobody yet knows how this goes. Your job tonight is to set a tone — warm, honest, no-phones, no-dumb-questions — and to plant the word **covenant** in every kid's head before they walk out.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
**Church-specific references.** The script below mentions **J.C.** (the program director — swap for your own point of contact) and the **APC App** (the Allentown Presbyterian Church app — swap for whatever group-chat tool you actually use). Everything else is church-agnostic.
{{< /callout >}}

## Before class

- Write down attendance and share it with J.C.
- Pens and slips of paper ready for the closing reflection
- Bibles for the Joshua 24 reading
- Whiteboard or phone notes ready for the ground-rules list

## Opening — Go-Around

**Have everyone share** (including teachers):

- Name
- School they attend
- Highs and lows from the summer
- Do you have any siblings? What order do you fall in?
  - What's cool about that? What's not so cool about that?

## Confirmation Is a Journey

**Say:**

> Confirmation is not meant to be like school, or a class — it's meant to be a **journey of faith** together. It's a journey where we learn and practice together what it means to be a Christian and to follow Jesus.
>
> At the end of confirmation, if you so choose, you'll have the opportunity to make a public profession of faith and to join the church.
>
> It's also a journey we take **together.**

Have everyone join the APC Confirmation group on the APC App. (See the [Group Chat Setup](../../running-the-year/group-chat/) page for the how-and-why.)

## Ground Rules

We also want to create a few ground rules. **Teacher, write these down in a note in your phone.** (Examples: *listen louder than you speak, no putdowns…*)

**Ask:**

> What would be some good ground rules?

Teachers, **make sure to** add:

- Everyone opens or closes in prayer at least once
- What happens at Confirmation stays at Confirmation — unless it's a danger to you or someone else
- No question is a dumb question
- No phones

## Would You Rather?

**Ask:**

> Would you rather have to break a promise, or have a promise broken to you? Explain.

## Read — Joshua 24

**Say:**

> Today we're going to briefly look at a passage in the Bible in the book of Joshua that deals with commitments and promises from God to God's people, the nation of Israel. The Bible is the story of God's people that leads us to Jesus.
>
> Joshua was the leader of Israel after Moses. The Israelites were enslaved by Pharaoh in Egypt. Then God used Moses to lead them out of slavery, but they spent forty years wandering in the desert.
>
> Finally they come to a new place called Canaan, but Moses dies and Joshua becomes their leader. The people in the land of Canaan follow other gods and idols — they have statues they believe are connected to other gods, and they worship those statues. Israel would fall into this same practice in the future.
>
> But before this can happen, God uses Joshua to speak to the people about their covenant with God and how they should live.

*Pass around Bibles. Choose a few readers and read:*

- **Joshua 24:1–3, 13–15, 22–25**

**Ask:**

1. ***What do you think a covenant is? (v.25)***
   - **A covenant is a promise for the sake of relationship. God makes a covenant with us to love us and always be with us. We fulfill that covenant — that promise — by loving God and loving others.**
2. ***What do you think it means to "put away the foreign gods that are among you, and incline your hearts to the Lord, the God of Israel" (v.23)?***
   - What are some examples of foreign gods today?
3. ***What are some things we can do to be Joshua for each other and stand firm in our commitment to love God and love others throughout this confirmation journey?***
4. ***What promises do you think you'll be asked to make at Confirmation?***

## The Big Four

**Say:**

> Over the next several months we'll be exploring the **big four questions** that you'll be asked on the day of your confirmation in June:

1. Trusting in the gracious mercy of God, do you turn from the ways of sin and renounce evil and its power in the world?
2. Who is your Lord and Savior?
3. Will you be Christ's faithful disciple, obeying his Word and showing his love?
4. Will you devote yourself to the church's teaching and fellowship, to the breaking of bread and the prayers?

## Written Reflection

**Pass out pens and slips of paper.** Write the following two questions on the board and have students answer them. They can — but don't have to — write their names on the slips. Collect the slips afterwards.

- What is one thing you want to get out of your confirmation journey?
- What questions do you have about God?

**Ask:**

> Is anyone open to sharing what they wrote?

## Close

**Say:**

> The next class is next week and your parents are expected to attend. *(J.C. will email parents as well.)*

**Pray out together as a class.**
