**Part B of Session 5 · Salvation.**

## Before class

- Confirmation ground rules; ask who will open in prayer next week
- Bibles for all
- Whiteboard + markers
- Screen / laptop for YouTube videos
- Index cards with the three passage groups written on them (see *Breakout Groups* below)

## Opening

**Ask:**

- How was everyone's Christmas?
- What is one Christmas tradition your family has?

## Recap

**Ask:** What does *Savior* mean? *(Invite synonyms; write them on the board.)*

**Write on the board:** *"Who is your Savior?"*

**Say:**

> The things of the world all promise to give us a solution to different problems.

**Ask:** In the Presbyterian religion, what is *our* promise? *(Wait for answers.)*

**Say:**

> Our promise is **GRACE.** Grace fully known in the love and sacrifice of Jesus.

Write on the board:

> **G**od's **R**iches **A**t **C**hrist's **E**xpense

**Say:**

> Presbyterians believe that God promises a solution to our brokenness and the brokenness of the world in the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ. We call that **salvation.** Salvation is God's gift to save us from all that is against God and give us abundant life here and now, *and* in eternity.

## Salvation Is Bigger Than Heaven and Hell

**Say:**

> Considering our eternal destiny is only *one* aspect of salvation. The Bible says a wide variety of things about that subject, but we can break it down into three:

1. The Bible says that **God chooses** those who are saved.
2. The Bible says that **when we choose God,** we are saved.
3. The Bible says that **all will be saved.**

**Say:**

> Those things live in tension — and we are called to **embrace the mystery.**
>
> What we know for sure is that salvation comes through Christ, and that we experience salvation through our relationship with him. But the idea of salvation in the Bible is bigger and broader than just thinking about heaven or hell.

## Read — Ephesians 2

Hand out Bibles. Have the confirmands turn to **Ephesians 2.**

*Ask a volunteer to read aloud verses 1–3.*

**Ask:**

- Why does Paul tell readers they were *dead?*
- What kind of death is Paul talking about?

*Ask a volunteer to read aloud verses 4–7.*

**Ask:**

- How does God change people in Christ, and why?
  - *Out of love, God saves people, makes them alive with Christ, and restores relationship with God through Christ.*

*Ask a volunteer to read aloud verses 8–10.*

**Ask:**

- What do humans need to do to receive God's saving love?
- Based on these verses, what do you think *grace* means?
- If our good works cannot save us, why do them?

## Breakout Groups — Salvation in Three Keys

Break into groups. Give each group an index card with a salvation theme + passages and the question set. Each group reads, discusses, and presents.

### Group 1 — Salvation means **freedom**

Read **Exodus 3:1–10:**

- What did Moses and the Hebrew people need to be saved (freed) from?
- In what ways is this a story of salvation?
- What does the story tell us about the heart of God?

Read **Luke 19:1–10** (Zacchaeus):

- What did Zacchaeus need to be saved (freed) from?
- In what ways is this a story of salvation?
- What does the story tell us about the heart of God?

### Group 2 — Salvation means **healing and wholeness**

Read **2 Kings 5:1–15** (Naaman):

- What did Naaman need to be saved (healed) from?
- In what ways is this a story of salvation?
- What does the story tell us about the heart of God?

Read **Mark 2:1–12** (the man lowered through the roof):

- What did the man who was paralyzed need to be saved (healed) from?
- In what ways is this a story of salvation?
- What does the story tell us about the heart of God?

### Group 3 — Salvation means **forgiveness**

Read **Matthew 18:21–35** (the unmerciful servant):

- What did the men who were in debt need to be saved (forgiven) from?
- In what ways is this a story of salvation?
- What does the story tell us about the heart of God?

Read **Luke 15:11–32** (the prodigal son):

- What did the prodigal need to be saved (forgiven) from?
- In what ways is this a story of salvation?
- What does the story tell us about the heart of God?

## Common Thread

After groups present, discuss: **what's the common theme across all three keys?**

**Ask:** What questions do you still have about salvation?

## Optional — Humanity and Divinity of Christ

If there's time: get into a discussion about **Christ's humanity** and **Christ's divinity.**

Draw a continuum on the board. Ask confirmands to name things that fall on either side.

**Say:**

> As fully human, Christ *gets us* and understands our struggle. As fully God, Christ has *the power* to save us from sin in the world.

## Close

**Finish with prayer.**
