**The end-of-year celebration.**

The week before Confirmation Sunday, families, mentors, and pastors gather for dessert. Each confirmand is introduced by a teacher, lights a candle, and reads their statement of faith aloud to the room. At the end, the elders vote to receive the class into membership.

## Who Is Invited

- **Students** and their **parents / siblings**
- **Confirmation teachers**
- **Session** (the congregation's elders)
- **Pastors**
- **Confirmation director**
- **HS youth leader**
- Any **immediate family** the students want to invite

Confirmation teachers invite students and families. The confirmation director notifies the clerk of session by the **end of April** so session can be invited. **Session brings the dessert.**

## Prep

- **All statements of faith are ready to be read.** Timeline:
  - End of April — begin distributing statements of faith for draft
  - 2nd-to-last week in May — statements of faith **due**
  - Last week in May — **final** statements (must be edited and locked by this date)
  - Voice recordings of statements submitted for the Confirmation Sunday slideshow
- **Each teacher owns a few confirmands** to introduce at the Dessert
- **Retrieve full names** of confirmands and parents for the session vote and the worship service

## Logistics

- **Dress:** semi-formal / business casual
- **Arrive:** 6:15pm to help set up
- **Event:** 7–8:30pm (may run to 9pm)
- **Location:** your youth-ministry space or the church sanctuary

## Supplies

- Dessert *(session brings)*
- **Christ light** *(Paschal candle)* on the central table
- **Tea lights** — one per confirmand
- A central table with **matches, tea lights, and the Christ light,** where confirmands read
- **8 tables** with white tablecloths
- **Microphone** for statements

## Run-of-Show

1. **Host opens** — welcomes everyone, explains what's going to happen.
2. **Introduce the teachers.** A teacher begins introducing the confirmands (teachers alternate).
   - Each teacher says a few words about each confirmand.
   - The confirmand is invited up, **lights a tea candle from the Christ light** *(put the match at the top of the flame, then pick up the tea light to light it — symbolizing the light of Christ in all of us),* and **reads their statement of faith** at the microphone.
3. After all statements have been read, the host opens the floor to **questions** from session.
4. **Session votes** to receive the confirmands as members of the church.
5. Host talks briefly about **Confirmation Sunday** — arrival time, dress, seating, what the confirmands will do.
6. **Close in prayer.**

{{< callout type="info" >}}
**Mentor introductions.** Each teacher writes a brief intro for each student they own — usually 3–4 sentences. Name the strengths you've seen in them over the year; a specific memory or two; one thing you're praying for them going forward. Keep it honest and short. Example structure:

> *"[Confirmand], I first met you at [event / moment]. This year I've watched you [specific observation]. What has struck me most is [quality or trait]. I've been blessed to [something specific you've shared — a game, a conversation, a prayer]. I can't wait to see how God uses you."*
{{< /callout >}}

## Confirmation Sunday (next day)

- **Students and families arrive by 9am.** The students are leading worship, and families will want to get a seat before the service starts.
- Students sit with families, dispersed equally on either aisle.
- After the sermon, the **pastor invites the confirmands to stand and affirm their faith.**
- If any students are being **baptized,** that happens here.
- **Parents lay hands** and pray over their students as the students kneel.
- **Teachers anoint** each student's head with oil.

See the [Children & Youth Sunday](../children-and-youth-sunday/) page for the full service structure.
