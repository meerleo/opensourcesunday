**Confirmation Sunday, led by confirmands and children.**

Instead of the pastoral staff running the whole service, the confirmands and children do. Confirmands preach, read Scripture, and run the tech; the children do call-to-worship, confession, the Lord's Prayer, and the doxology. It's the liturgical capstone of the year.

## Roles

Assign each role to a specific student by the end of April so they have time to prep. Roles from our recent services:

- **Greeters** (2–3 confirmands + children) — welcome people as they arrive; hand out bulletins. These students can also be the offering collectors.
- **Welcome / Announcements** (2 confirmands) — the opening welcome; include a VBS or announcement slot for the pre-K / kindergarten group.
- **Call to Worship** — led by the children.
- **Song leaders / band** — the youth band (or a guest musician) leads songs.
- **Confession / Pardon** — led by the children.
- **Children's message** — given by the children (plan ~8 minutes).
- **Scripture readings** (1–2 confirmands) — see the suggested list below.
- **Sermon** (1–2 confirmands) — one 10–15-minute message or two 5–7-minute messages.
- **Offering** — the greeters repeat as offering collectors.
- **Doxology** — led by the children (e.g. 2nd graders).
- **Prayers** — children sing the Lord's Prayer with the congregation.
- **Sending song** — often *Love the Lord Your God* (Lincoln Brewster) with a video.
- **Benediction** (1–2 confirmands).
- **Tech team** (1–3 confirmands) — video / slides / sound, includes at least one practice Sunday.

## The Welcome

Three practical notes the students should internalize:

- **Smile.**
- **Speak loudly and clearly.**
- **Take turns** — don't talk over each other.

Two Scriptures that ground the welcome:

> ***Romans 12:15 — "Live in harmony with one another. Do not be proud, but be willing to associate with people of low position. Do not be conceited."***

> ***John 13:20 — "Very truly I tell you, whoever accepts anyone I send accepts me; and whoever accepts me accepts the one who sent me."***

### Welcome script template

> *Good morning, everyone. My name is ______, and ______ and ______ are your worship hosts for the morning. Today is Children & Youth Sunday, meaning that everything is being led and has been created by the children and teens of our church.*
>
> - *First, we would like to invite up the kindergarteners to make a special announcement...*
> - *Again, welcome to those in the sanctuary and joining us online. We are so glad you are here, especially if this is your first time with us. If you're new, please fill out a connect card and put it in the offering plate later in the service. We would love to get to know you better.*
> - *— insert any congregation-wide announcements: annual meetings, upcoming events, hospitality items, etc. —*
> - *And lastly, an important word from our Pre-K class...*
>   - *(As they hold up a sign, the congregation says "**Welcome to church! Let us stand and greet one another.**")*

## Suggested Scripture Readings

For the scripture-reading slot, lean toward passages on the **authority and living power of Scripture** (fits the "Will you devote yourself to the church's teaching?" vow):

- **2 Timothy 3:16–17** — *"All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the servant of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work."*
- **Hebrews 4:12** — *"For the word of God is alive and active..."*
- **Romans 15:4** — *"For everything that was written in the past was written to teach us, so that through the endurance taught in the Scriptures and the encouragement they provide we might have hope."*
- **John 17:17** — *"Sanctify them by the truth; your word is truth."*
- **John 1:14** — *"The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us..."*
- **Deuteronomy 6:4–9** — the Shema.
- **Psalm 119:105** — *"Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path."*

## The Student Sermon

One confirmand (or two, splitting a 15-minute message into two 7-minute pieces) preaches. Use a simple framework:

### The shape

- **Me** — something I struggle with / wrestle with.
- **We** — something we *all* struggle with.
- **God** — here is the solution.
- **You** — what you need to do.
- **We** — what if everybody did this? *(The vision.)*

### The worksheet

Send the student preacher these five questions, at least a month out:

1. What do you want them to know?
2. Why do you want them to know it?
3. What do you want them to do?
4. Why do you want them to do it?
5. How can I help them remember it?

### The outline

- **Text** — what passage will be the focal point of the message?
- **Focus** — one line about the purpose of the sermon. *If you can't say it in one line, it isn't focused.*
- **Trouble** — what is the human burden or struggle we are trying to overcome?
- **Grace** — how does God help us overcome that burden? What is the good news of what God is doing or will do?
- **Your Move** — what action do you want them to take?

**Coaching tips for the student preacher:** write it out word-for-word first, then cut 30%. Read it out loud to a parent or mentor. Practice once standing in front of the pulpit on the Saturday before. Keep it shorter than you think.

Two past examples. Names redacted; the structure is the useful part.

### Year A (9:30am service)

1. Greeters + offering collectors — 2 confirmands + children
2. **Welcome / Announcements** — 2 confirmands; Pre-K / K kids' VBS announcement
3. **Call to Worship** — children
4. Song *(upbeat)* — youth band
5. **Confession / Pardon** — children

8. **Sermon** — 1–2 confirmands, *"Journey of Faith"*

## Confirmation Rite

After the sermon, the pastor invites the confirmands to stand and affirm their faith:

1. **Baptisms** (if any students are being baptized at Confirmation).
2. The class answers the **Big Four Questions** together (see [Session 1A](../../sessions/01a-kickoff/#the-big-four)).
3. The class says the **Apostles' Creed** in unison.
4. **Parents lay hands** and pray over their students as the students kneel.
5. **Teachers anoint** each student's head with oil.
6. The class is **presented to the congregation** as new active members.
