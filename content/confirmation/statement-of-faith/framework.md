**How to help a 9th grader write a statement of faith.**

Three pieces of scaffolding we put in front of students during the last weeks of Confirmation. They aren't a checklist — they're a **starter kit** for students who've never had to write anything like this before.

## 1. The Apostles' Creed

The Apostles' Creed is the oldest, shortest, most widely-shared summary of Christian faith in the world. Every denomination that uses it says the same words. A student's statement doesn't have to quote the creed — but it should *address* what the creed addresses.

Use it as a **checklist of territory.** If a student's statement skips one of these moves entirely, ask them why.

- **Who is God the Father?** Maker of heaven and earth.
- **Who is Jesus?** His only Son, our Lord — conceived, born, suffered, crucified, died, buried, descended, rose, ascended, seated, coming back.
- **Who is the Holy Spirit?** The Spirit of God at work in the world.
- **What is the church?** The holy catholic (*universal*) church. The communion of saints.
- **What does Christ offer?** Forgiveness of sins. Resurrection of the body. Life everlasting.

## 2. The Big 4 Questions

A simpler framework. Each student writes a paragraph on each:

1. **Who is God to you?**
2. **Who is Jesus to you?**
3. **Who is the Holy Spirit to you?**
4. **What is the church to you — and what is *your* place in it?**

Some students will finish the Big 4 and realize they *have* a statement of faith. Others will use it as an outline to write a longer piece from.

## 3. The Writing Guide

A page-by-page walk through the drafting process. Use this as a worksheet the student fills in before they start prose.

### Page 1 — Who am I?

- What's my name, grade, and a couple of things I care about?
- Who is important to me in this life (family, friends, a mentor)?
- What's an experience of worship, nature, or service that has made me feel close to God?

### Page 2 — Who is God to me?

- How would I describe God to someone who had never heard the word?
- What has God done that I see in my own life?
- When has it been hard to trust God? What's helped?

### Page 3 — Who is Jesus to me?

- Who is Jesus *in the Bible* — what do the gospels say about him?
- Who is Jesus *for me* — what has he done for me, and what am I learning to let him do?
- Which of the Big Four vows do I especially want to say *yes* to?

### Page 4 — Who is the Holy Spirit to me?

- How have I seen the Spirit at work — in me, in my family, in the church?
- What are the gifts I believe the Spirit has given me?

### Page 5 — What is the church, and what's my place in it?

- What do I love about the church?
- Where do I want to serve?
- What do I want my membership to *do* in the world?

### Page 6 — My confession

Write out a closing paragraph that begins *"I believe ..."* and ends with *"... and I want to be a faithful member of this church."*

## Editing Pass

Before the student reads aloud at the Confirmation Dessert, a teacher or mentor does a light editing pass:

- **Honesty check** — is this actually what they believe, or what they think we want to hear?
- **Voice check** — does this sound like *them,* or like a Hallmark card?
- **Apostles' Creed check** — have they at least *touched* God, Jesus, the Spirit, and the church?
- **Length check** — 2–3 minutes read aloud is ideal. Trim gently if it's longer than 4 minutes.

{{< callout type="warning" >}}
**Do not rewrite for the student.** An edited statement of faith is only worth what the student actually believes of it. Query, prompt, suggest — but let the words stay theirs.
{{< /callout >}}
