**Scripture:** Matthew 4:23–5:5
**Series:** [Sermon on the Mount](../)

## Behind the Curtain
*What do I need to know before teaching?*

By the end of Matthew 4, Jesus has become the most talked-about rabbi in the region. He's been traveling all over Galilee, teaching in synagogues, proclaiming the good news of the kingdom, and healing every kind of sickness and pain. Crowds from Galilee, the Decapolis, Jerusalem, Judea, and beyond the Jordan are chasing him down. Then, in Matthew 5:1, he sees the crowd, walks up a mountainside, sits down (the posture of a teacher in that day), and begins the most famous sermon ever preached.

Before Jesus says a single word about how to live, he tells us *who God is cheering for*. The "Beatitudes" (from the Latin *beatus* — blessed, happy, flourishing) are not a checklist of behaviors to unlock God's favor. They are Jesus announcing that God's kingdom runs on an **upside-down scoreboard**. The people the world calls losers — the poor in spirit, the mourners, the meek — God calls blessed. That would have sounded as scandalous then as it does now.

For middle schoolers, this is enormous. They live in a world where "blessed" almost always means *rich, pretty, followed, talented, winning*. Jesus just took a sharpie to that whole list.

This first lesson is the setup for the entire four-week series. We're asking one big question: *Who does Jesus say is blessed?* Then we'll walk through the first three Beatitudes — **poor in spirit, those who mourn, the meek** — so students can hear the pattern before we go deeper in the coming weeks.

{{< callout type="info" >}}
*Do our students actually believe Jesus when he says the people the world overlooks are the ones God calls blessed? Or do they, like the rest of us, secretly keep score the same way the world does?*
{{< /callout >}}

## Prop Department
*What supplies do I need?*

- Bibles, one per student
- Whiteboard or large paper + markers (two colors)
- Index cards — one per student
- Pens/pencils
- Christ light / candle + matches (for opening)
- Optional: printed photos of a trophy, a crown, a hospital bed, an empty lunch table, a kid sitting alone — anything that helps contrast "worldly blessed" with "Jesus blessed"

## Directing the Scene
*What learning activities might we do?*

### Start with Prayer

Light the Christ light. Invite a student to lead a short popcorn prayer. Thank God for the gift of the group, and ask the Holy Spirit to open hearts to the words of Jesus tonight.

### Catch Their Attention — The Scoreboard

**Before the lesson:**
Write across the top of the whiteboard in big letters: **"WHO IS BLESSED?"**

**During the lesson:**
Say:
> Raise your hand if you've ever heard someone say *"I'm blessed"* — on a post, in a song, on a jersey, anywhere. When people say that, what do they usually mean?

Let students shout out answers. Write them on the board in one color. (You'll probably get: rich, healthy, popular, good family, winning, famous, lots of followers, talented, pretty, etc.)

Then say:
> Okay — same question, but now in *Jesus'* version. If Jesus wrote the list, who do you think he'd put on it?

Don't give the answer yet. Let them guess. Write their guesses in a **different color.** Then say:
> Hold onto that. Let's read what Jesus actually says — and see how close we got.

### Read and Interpret — Matthew 4:23–5:5

**Intro for the youth:** Before reading, say:
> Over the next four weeks we're going to be in the most famous sermon Jesus ever preached — something called **the Sermon on the Mount.** Three whole chapters of Jesus teaching us how to live in God's kingdom. Tonight we're reading the very beginning — who showed up, where Jesus sat down, and the very first words out of his mouth.

Read **Matthew 4:23–5:5** together. Go around the circle; each student reads 1–2 verses. Anyone who doesn't want to read can say *"pass"* — no pressure.

**Discuss:**

1. ***Before Jesus even says a word, what kind of crowd is showing up?*** *(v. 23–25)* — Sick people. Demon-possessed. People in pain. People from everywhere. Not the cool kids.
2. ***Jesus sees this crowd, goes up the mountain, and sits down. Why does that matter?*** — In that day, a teacher *stood* to read the Scripture but *sat* to teach with authority. Jesus is about to drop something huge.
3. ***Jesus uses the word "blessed" over and over. Based on these first three lines, how is his idea of "blessed" different from the one on our board?***
4. ***"Poor in spirit" — what do you think that means?*** — Spiritually empty-handed. Knowing you don't have it all together. The opposite of proud.
5. ***"Those who mourn" — why on earth would Jesus call grieving people blessed?*** — Because God is the God who *comforts*. Grief is not the end of the story for people who belong to him.
6. ***"The meek" — is meek the same as weak?*** — Nope. Meek = strength under control. Like a warhorse that's been trained. Think Moses. Think Jesus.

**Say:**
> Here's what Jesus is doing. He is flipping the world's scoreboard upside down. The world cheers for people who have it all together. Jesus cheers for people who know they don't. The world runs *away* from people who are grieving. Jesus runs *toward* them. The world worships the loudest person in the room. Jesus blesses the one who doesn't need to be.
>
> For the next three weeks, we're going to hear eight of these upside-down blessings. And they are going to change how we look at our school, our team, our lunch table — and our own hearts.

### Ponder the Possibilities

Discuss together:

1. ***Looking back at our board — how different is Jesus' list from what the world calls "blessed"?***
2. ***Who in your school do most people ignore or laugh at — who Jesus might actually be calling "blessed"?***
3. ***Have you ever felt like one of the people on Jesus' list? (Poor in spirit, grieving, small, overlooked?) What would it mean to you to hear Jesus call you "blessed" right there?***
4. ***Why do you think Jesus started his biggest sermon with this list? What is he trying to do to us before he teaches us anything else?***

### Turn Toward Action — Name One

Hand each student an index card. Say:
> On this card, write the name of one person in your life who the world doesn't cheer for — someone who fits Jesus' list more than the world's list. You don't have to show anyone. Just write the name.
>
> Slip it in your pocket, your phone case, or your Bible. Your job this week: pray for that person every day. And do *one* thing — literally one thing — that tells them they matter. Say hi. Save them a seat. Text them. Invite them. Anything.

End with prayer. Invite students to pray — out loud or silently — for the name on their card. Close with:

> Jesus, thank you that your blessing doesn't depend on the scoreboard of the world. Give us your eyes this week. Amen.
