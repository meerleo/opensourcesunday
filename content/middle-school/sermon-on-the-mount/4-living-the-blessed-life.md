**Scripture:** Matthew 5:1–12 *(full Beatitudes)*
**Series:** [Sermon on the Mount](../)

## Behind the Curtain
*What do I need to know before teaching?*

This is the wrap-up. For three weeks, students have climbed the mountain with Jesus. They've heard him flip the scoreboard. They've looked underneath — at what they crave, how they give mercy, who they really are. They've been challenged to step into conflict and to stand up when it costs something.

Tonight we zoom all the way back out and read the **whole** Beatitudes as one piece. And here's the big reveal that holds the whole series together:

**Every single Beatitude is a portrait of Jesus himself.**

- He was **poor in spirit** — the King of glory born in a feeding trough.
- He **mourned** — he wept at Lazarus' tomb, he wept over Jerusalem.
- He was **meek** — "gentle and humble in heart" (Matt. 11:29), riding on a donkey.
- He **hungered and thirsted for righteousness** — his food was "to do the will of him who sent me" (John 4:34).
- He was **merciful** — "Father, forgive them" (Luke 23:34).
- He was **pure in heart** — the sinless one.
- He was the ultimate **peacemaker** — he "made peace through his blood, shed on the cross" (Col. 1:20).
- He was **persecuted** — even to death on a cross.

When Jesus said "blessed are…" he wasn't giving us eight random categories. He was describing the shape of the life he was about to live — and inviting us into it.

The second big idea: the Beatitudes are **bookended by the same promise.** Verse 3 and verse 10 both end with *"for theirs is the kingdom of heaven."* That's Jesus' way of saying: this isn't pie-in-the-sky someday — you are *already* in the kingdom the moment you step into this way of life.

This lesson pulls the series together and asks each student: *which Beatitude is God inviting you to lean into in this season of your life?*

## Center Stage
*What are the most important takeaways?*

{{< callout type="important" >}}
**The Beatitudes are the shape of Jesus' own life. He's not asking us to do anything he hasn't already done — he's inviting us to follow him into the blessed life, one step at a time.**
{{< /callout >}}

## Prop Department
*What supplies do I need?*

- Bibles, one per student
- Eight printed signs — one for each Beatitude — posted around the room **before** students arrive
- Commitment cards (index cards or printed templates — see Turn toward Action)
- Pens/pencils (one per student)
- Whiteboard or large paper
- Christ light / candle + matches
- Worship music cued up (band or track — something reflective)
- Optional: a small card or printed Beatitudes list for each student to take home

### The Eight Signs

Write one per sheet, in large letters:

1. **POOR IN SPIRIT** — *Theirs is the kingdom of heaven*
2. **MOURNING** — *They will be comforted*
3. **MEEK** — *They will inherit the earth*
4. **HUNGRY FOR RIGHTEOUSNESS** — *They will be filled*
5. **MERCIFUL** — *They will be shown mercy*
6. **PURE IN HEART** — *They will see God*
7. **PEACEMAKERS** — *They will be called children of God*
8. **PERSECUTED FOR RIGHTEOUSNESS** — *Theirs is the kingdom of heaven*

## Directing the Scene
*What learning activities might we do?*

### Start with Prayer

Light the Christ light. Invite the whole group to join in a popcorn prayer — *thank God for the last three weeks, ask him to speak clearly tonight.*

### Catch Their Attention — The Gallery Walk

**Before the lesson:**
Post the eight Beatitude signs around the room — spread them out on walls, tables, even the floor. Have the room set up so students can walk around freely.

**During the lesson:**
Say:
> For three weeks we've been climbing this mountain together. Tonight we're going to walk through *all eight* of the Beatitudes at once. Here's what I want you to do:
>
> Take a lap around the room. Read every sign. No talking yet. Just read.
>
> When you're done, come stand next to the Beatitude that **hits you the hardest** right now. Not the one that's easiest. The one you need the most. The one God is nudging you toward.

Put on music. Let them walk. Give it at least 3–4 minutes. When most have landed somewhere, call the group back in.

Ask a few volunteers: ***Why did you land where you did?*** No one has to share, but some will want to. This is holy stuff — don't rush it.

### Read and Interpret — Matthew 5:1–12

**Intro for the youth:** Before reading, say:
> Tonight we're reading the whole thing — all eight Beatitudes — straight through. Listen for a pattern. Listen for where Jesus promises something. And listen for where you hear Jesus describing himself.

Read **Matthew 5:1–12** together. One student reads it all, slowly, while everyone else listens with eyes closed. Or go around and have each student read one verse.

**Discuss:**

1. ***What stood out to you hearing all eight together?***
2. ***Do you notice the pattern?*** — Every one has the word "blessed" and every one has a promise. (Blessed are \_\_\_, for they \_\_\_.)
3. ***Look at the first and last Beatitudes (verses 3 and 10). What do they have in common?*** — Same promise: *theirs is the kingdom of heaven.* Jesus bookends the whole list with it. He's saying: this life isn't a waiting game. You're *already* in the kingdom.
4. ***Here's a wild question: which of these Beatitudes describes Jesus himself?***

Pause on that last one. Walk them through it together. Write on the whiteboard:

- Poor in spirit → *Born in a feeding trough.*
- Mourned → *Wept at Lazarus' tomb.*
- Meek → *"Gentle and humble in heart."*
- Hungered for righteousness → *"My food is to do the will of my Father."*
- Merciful → *"Father, forgive them."*
- Pure in heart → *The sinless one.*
- Peacemaker → *Made peace through the cross.*
- Persecuted → *All the way to the cross.*

**Say:**
> Here's the big thing I want you to see tonight. Jesus wasn't giving us eight random rules. He was describing **the shape of his own life**. Every Beatitude is a portrait of him.
>
> So when Jesus says "blessed are the poor in spirit…blessed are the merciful…blessed are the peacemakers…" he is literally saying: **follow me into the blessed life.** *I've already lived it. Come with me.*
>
> That changes everything. This isn't a list of impossible standards. It's an invitation. Jesus is not standing at the top of the mountain yelling at us to climb. He's climbing it with us.

### Ponder the Possibilities

Discuss together:

1. ***Which Beatitude surprised you most over these four weeks? Why?***
2. ***Which Beatitude is God inviting you into right now, in this season of your life?***
3. ***Remember the three truths painted on our walls at Crossroads? "In Jesus Christ, you are loved, you belong, and you have been created to make a real difference." How do the Beatitudes connect to those three?***
4. ***If you lived the Beatitudes out loud this year — at school, on your team, in your family — what do you think would change? About you? About them?***

### Turn Toward Action — One Beatitude, One Season

Hand every student a commitment card. Have a student read a template like this, or print one:

> **MY BEATITUDE**
>
> In this season, I believe God is inviting me into the Beatitude of ___________________________.
>
> One thing I'm going to do this month to lean into it:
> ____________________________________________
>
> One person I'm going to ask to pray for me / walk with me in this:
> ____________________________________________
>
> **Signed:** ____________________________________

Give students space and quiet to fill it out. Play worship music.

Then say:
> You get to keep this card. Put it in your Bible. Put it on your mirror. Put it in your locker. And find the person you wrote down and **actually ask them** — this week — if they'll pray for you.

**Close in a big circle.** Invite students to hold the card as you pray:

> Jesus, every single one of these Beatitudes is *you*. Thank you that you don't just tell us how to live — you lived it first, all the way to the cross, all the way out of the grave. Thank you that your kingdom is already here for the poor in spirit, the mourners, the merciful, the brave. Help us follow you this season into the blessed life. Amen.

**Close the series.** Before students leave, stand at the door and say each student's name and bless them:

> *"[Name], you are loved beyond your imagining. You belong no matter what. You have been created to make a real difference. Go be blessed."*

That's how we end this series. That's the blessed life.
