**Scripture:** Matthew 5:17–48
**Series:** [Sermon on the Mount](../)

## Behind the Curtain
*What do I need to know before teaching?*

After the Beatitudes (Lesson 1) and salt-and-light (Lesson 2), Jesus goes in. He makes a huge statement: *"Do not think that I have come to abolish the Law or the Prophets; I have come not to abolish them but to fulfill them."* (v.17) Then, six times in a row, he pulls the same move: **"You have heard that it was said… but I say to you…"**

The six antitheses are:

1. **Murder → anger** (vv.21–26) — Don't just not murder. Don't even nurse a grudge. Go reconcile.
2. **Adultery → lust** (vv.27–30) — Don't just not cheat. Don't even *look* at a person as a thing.
3. **Divorce** (vv.31–32) — Don't treat marriage as disposable. *(Worth knowing, but for middle school this can be mentioned briefly without camping out.)*
4. **Oaths** (vv.33–37) — Don't need fancy vows. Be the kind of person whose *yes* means yes.
5. **Eye for eye → turn the other cheek** (vv.38–42) — Don't play the revenge game. Break the cycle.
6. **Love neighbor, hate enemy → love your enemies** (vv.43–48) — The wildest one. Love the people who actively hurt you.

The climax is v.48: *"Be perfect, therefore, as your heavenly Father is perfect."* The Greek word for "perfect" there is *teleios* — it means **mature, complete, whole**. Not flawless. Jesus isn't telling middle schoolers to be sinless. He's telling them to grow into the whole-people God made them to be.

**This lesson's big move:** Jesus isn't raising the bar; he's going underneath it. The world's idea of being good is about behavior — don't cross the line. Jesus cares about the heart — don't even want to cross it. That's not harder religion. That's a new heart.

Three antitheses will give middle schoolers plenty: **anger, guarding the eyes, loving enemies.** Save divorce, oaths, and retaliation for teacher notes — you can reference them briefly, but don't stretch the lesson thin.

## Prop Department
*What supplies do I need?*

- Bibles, one per student
- Whiteboard or large paper + markers
- Blue painter's tape (optional — to mark a "waterline" on the wall for the iceberg illustration)
- Index cards — one per student
- Pens/pencils
- Christ light / candle + matches (for opening)

## Directing the Scene
*What learning activities might we do?*

### Start with Prayer

Light the Christ light. Invite a student to lead in prayer. Tonight's teaching goes to the heart — pray that the Holy Spirit would make us brave enough to go there with Jesus.

### Catch Their Attention — Surface vs. Heart (The Iceberg)

**Before the lesson:**
On the whiteboard, draw a big iceberg. Draw a wavy waterline across the middle. Label the top third **SURFACE** and the bottom two-thirds **HEART**.

**During the lesson:**
Say:
> Every day of your life, two of you are going at the same time. There's *surface-you* — what people see, what you post, how you act — and there's *heart-you* — what's actually going on underneath.

Ask the group:
- *Give me some things that live on the "surface."* (How I dress. The GPA. The followers. The team I'm on. How I act at youth group.)
- *Now things that live "below the waterline."* (Anger. Jealousy. What I look at when no one's watching. Grudges. Anxiety. What I actually believe about myself.)

Write their answers in the right zones.

Say:
> Most rules in the world — at school, at home, online — only care about the top half. Don't get caught. Don't cross the line. Look fine. Jesus walks into the Sermon on the Mount and says: *I care about the whole iceberg.*

### Read and Interpret — Matthew 5:17–48

**Intro for the youth:** Before reading, say:
> Jesus is about to do something wild. He's going to take rules that everyone in the room already knew — rules from the Old Testament — and he's going to push each one *deeper*. Watch for the phrase **"You have heard it said… but I say to you."** He does this six times. We're going to camp on three of them.

Read **Matthew 5:17–20** to set the stage. Jesus is not throwing out the Law. He's *fulfilling* it.

Discuss briefly:
1. ***Some people think Jesus came to scrap the Old Testament. What does he actually say?*** — *"I have not come to abolish them but to fulfill them."*
2. ***What do you think it means for Jesus to *fulfill* the Law?*** — He completes it. He shows us what it was always pointing to. He makes it more than rule-following — he makes it love.

Then take three antitheses.

#### Antithesis 1 — Anger (Matt 5:21–24)

Read it. Ask:
1. ***What's the old rule?*** — Don't murder.
2. ***What does Jesus add?*** — Don't even nurse anger. Don't call people names. Reconcile.
3. ***Jesus actually says: if you're bringing a gift to God and you remember your brother has something against you, leave the gift and go make it right first. Why would Jesus put reconciliation BEFORE worship?*** — Because you can't worship a God of love while you're hating your brother. Real worship and real relationships are tied together.

#### Antithesis 2 — Lust / Guarding the Eyes (Matt 5:27–30)

Read it. Say up front — this one can feel awkward. That's okay. Jesus is taking it seriously; so are we.

Ask:
1. ***What's the old rule?*** — Don't cheat on your spouse.
2. ***What does Jesus push past the rule to?*** — What your eyes are doing. What your heart is doing before a single action happens.
3. ***In 5:29–30 Jesus talks about cutting off the hand or plucking out the eye. Is he being literal?*** — No. He's using shock language to make a point: *take this seriously.* Whatever you're letting your eyes feed on is shaping your heart.
4. ***For a middle schooler, what does this passage mean practically?*** — What you scroll past. What shows you stream. What you DM. What you save. Jesus is saying: don't just avoid the "line" — guard what your eyes feed on.

#### Antithesis 3 — Love Your Enemies (Matt 5:43–48)

Read it. This is the wild one.

Ask:
1. ***What's the old rule?*** — Love your neighbor. Hate your enemy.
2. ***What does Jesus replace it with?*** — Love your enemies. Pray for those who persecute you.
3. ***Why does Jesus say this makes us like our Father?*** — Because God sends rain on the evil and the good. He gives sun to people who don't love him back. If we only love people who love us, we're doing nothing different than the world.
4. ***Who's an "enemy" in your life? Not someone trying to kill you — someone who's been unkind, who left you out, who made fun of you, who makes your life harder.***

Close the section with **v.48**:
> *"Be perfect, therefore, as your heavenly Father is perfect."*

Explain:
> The word "perfect" here doesn't mean *flawless*. It's a Greek word that means *mature, whole, complete.* Jesus is saying: grow up into the whole person your Father made you to be. Love like he loves. Be honest like he's honest. Stay patient like he stays patient. Not flawless. **Whole.**

**Say:**
> Every one of these teachings pushes past behavior to the heart. The world says, *"Just don't cross the line."* Jesus says, *"Don't even want to. Let me give you a new heart instead."*
>
> That's not harder religion. That's a different kind of religion. It's not about white-knuckling the rules. It's about a heart that's been made new from the inside.

### Ponder the Possibilities

Discuss together:

1. ***What's harder for you — not doing bad stuff, or not *wanting* to do it?***
2. ***Is there someone in your life right now who's gotten under your skin? What would "going to reconcile" look like?***
3. ***What are your eyes feeding on that you know is messing with your heart?***
4. ***Jesus said love your enemies. Who pops into your mind? What would it look like to pray for them by name this week?***
5. ***What's one "line" in your life that you don't cross… but maybe God cares more about what your heart is doing before it gets there?***

### Turn Toward Action — One Heart Move

Hand each student an index card. Say:
> Jesus wants more than your behavior. He wants your heart. Tonight, pick one heart-level move. ONE.

Write on the card:

1. **One person I'll reconcile with this week** (even if it's just a text).
2. **One thing I'll stop feeding my eyes** (an app limit, an unfollow, a scroll habit).
3. **One enemy I'll pray for by name** every day this week.

Pick *one* of those three. Write which one. Write the first concrete step.

Close with prayer. Pray that God would give this group new hearts — not just better behavior. Pray Ezekiel 36:26 out loud if you want a scripture to close with: *"I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit in you."*
