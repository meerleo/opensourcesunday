**Scripture:** Matthew 6:19–24
**Series:** [Sermon on the Mount](../)

## Behind the Curtain
*What do I need to know before teaching?*

Jesus has just finished talking about prayer (last week). Before he gets to *do not worry* (next week), he drops six of the most quoted verses in the whole sermon — and ties them together with a single idea: **what has your heart has your life.**

- **Verses 19–21 — Two Treasures.** *"Do not store up for yourselves treasures on earth, where moths and vermin destroy, and where thieves break in and steal. But store up for yourselves treasures in heaven… For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also."*
  - "Treasures on earth" — anything that can be eaten by moths, rusted by rain, or stolen by a thief. It decays. It runs out. It can be taken.
  - "Treasures in heaven" — things that *last.* Things God cares about. Love, faithfulness, mercy, the people you've helped, the forgiveness you've extended.
  - Verse 21 is the punchline. Where your treasure is, *there your heart will be also.* You follow your treasure like a magnet. Whatever you're collecting, you're becoming.
- **Verses 22–23 — The Lamp of the Eye.** *"The eye is the lamp of the body. If your eyes are healthy, your whole body will be full of light. But if your eyes are unhealthy, your whole body will be full of darkness."* Ancient metaphor: your eyes are the windows your body takes in light through. What you *look at* determines what fills you up.
- **Verse 24 — Two Masters.** *"No one can serve two masters… You cannot serve both God and money."* The word Jesus uses for money is *mammon* — it's personified almost like a god. Jesus isn't saying money is bad. He's saying money makes a terrible god. You can use money. You can't *serve* it.

**The thread:** Jesus is asking a penetrating question — *what are you living for?* What you store, what you look at, and who you serve all expose the same thing: your heart. And middle schoolers are already being shaped by what they're collecting, watching, and worshipping. They just don't always call it that.

## Prop Department
*What supplies do I need?*

- Bibles, one per student
- A physical "treasure pile" prop: a mix of stuff that decays/can-be-stolen (fake cash, a trophy, a phone, a jersey, a rusted old coin) and stuff that doesn't (a Bible, a card with a person's name, a cross, a photo of people serving)
- Whiteboard or large paper + markers
- Index cards — one per student
- Pens/pencils
- Christ light / candle + matches (for opening)

## Directing the Scene
*What learning activities might we do?*

### Start with Prayer

Light the Christ light. Invite a student to lead in prayer. Ask God to show each student where their treasure actually is tonight.

### Catch Their Attention — The Burning House

Say:
> Quick scenario. Your house is on fire. Everyone in your family is already out safe. The pets are out. You have exactly **sixty seconds** to run back in and grab ONE thing before the roof caves in. What do you grab?

Let every student answer. Write the answers on the whiteboard.

After the list is up, ask:
> Look at this list. What does it say about us that these are the things we'd run back in for?

Say:
> Whatever you would run back in for — that's a treasure. Tonight Jesus has something to say about what kind of treasure will actually last.

### Read and Interpret — Matthew 6:19–24

**Intro for the youth:** Before reading, say:
> Jesus is in the middle of the Sermon on the Mount. He just taught us how to pray. Now, before he gets to that famous passage about not worrying, he drops six verses that hit HARD. Listen for a single idea that runs through all three parts.

Read **Matthew 6:19–24** together. Go around the circle.

**Discuss:**

**Treasures (vv.19–21):**
1. ***What are "treasures on earth"? Why does Jesus say not to store them up?*** — Stuff that rots, rusts, or gets stolen. It runs out. It can be taken.
2. ***What are "treasures in heaven"? What lasts forever?*** — The people you helped. The mercy you showed. The forgiveness you gave. Your relationship with God. The ways you made God look good in the world.
3. ***Verse 21 — "Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also." What does that mean?*** — You follow your treasure. Whatever you're collecting, you start to love. Whatever you love, you start to become.

**The Lamp of the Eye (vv.22–23):**
4. ***Jesus says the eye is the lamp of the body. What do you think he means?*** — What you look at fills you up. If you feed your eyes garbage, your insides go dark. If you feed your eyes on good stuff, you fill with light.
5. ***For a middle schooler, what are your eyes on most? (Be honest.) What's filling you up right now?***

**Two Masters (v.24):**
6. ***Jesus says you can't serve TWO masters. Why not?*** — Because both want your whole heart. You end up loving one and hating the other.
7. ***Jesus specifically names ONE master that competes with God. What is it?*** — Money. And not just cash — "mammon," the whole *system* of chasing stuff to feel secure or important.
8. ***Is Jesus saying money is evil?*** — No. He's saying money makes a terrible *god*. You can **use** money. You can't **serve** it.

**Say:**
> Jesus is asking us one question three different ways tonight. *What are you living for?*
>
> What you **store up** shows your heart. What you **look at** fills your heart. Who you **serve** owns your heart. And you can't split your heart — the world says you can, Jesus says you can't.
>
> Here's what nobody's going to tell you at school: the stuff the world tells you to collect — the grades, the followers, the shoes, the spot on the team, the version of your body that's *in* this year — all of it will rust, break, fade, or get forgotten. Every bit of it. Jesus isn't saying those things are evil. He's saying they make *terrible* treasures. They can't hold your heart.
>
> But the things God asks us to store up — love for the kid nobody sits with, forgiveness for the one who hurt us, time with him, faithfulness when no one sees — that stuff **lasts forever.**

### Ponder the Possibilities

Discuss together:

1. ***Based on how you spend your time, your money, and your attention — what are you actually treasuring right now?***
2. ***What's one thing you look at a lot that you know is filling you with darkness, not light?***
3. ***Jesus says you can't serve God AND money. Is there another "master" you've been low-key serving? (Popularity? Grades? A sport? A person?)***
4. ***If your heart really follows your treasure — what do you want your heart to become over the next year?***
5. ***Verse 21 says it bluntly: your heart goes where your treasure is. What does that mean you need to change about what you're collecting?***

### Turn Toward Action — Move Some Treasure

Hand each student an index card. Say:
> Three quick moves this week. Pick the one God is putting in front of you.

1. **Move something up to heaven.** Spend time, money, or energy this week on someone who can't pay you back. A sibling. A classmate. A kid nobody sits with.
2. **Fast your eyes from something.** One app limit, one unfollow, one show you turn off. Something you know is filling you with darkness.
3. **Fire one of your masters.** Name a thing that's been running your life in secret — grades, a person's opinion, followers. Out loud, tell God you're not serving it anymore. Ask him to be your one master instead.

Write which one you picked and the first step.

Close with prayer. Read **Matt 6:21** out loud one more time on your way out:
> *"For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also."*
