**Scripture:** Matthew 7:24–29
**Series:** [Sermon on the Mount](../)

## Behind the Curtain
*What do I need to know before teaching?*

This is the capstone. For eight weeks we've been climbing the mountain with Jesus — the Beatitudes, salt and light, the heart of the Law, prayer, treasure, trust, judgment, and asking. Tonight Jesus steps up to close his own sermon, and the way he wraps it is *devastatingly* simple: a parable about two builders.

**Matthew 7:24–27** — both builders hear the same words. One puts them into practice and builds on the rock. The other hears but doesn't act and builds on the sand. The storm comes for both. One house stands. The other falls with a crash.

A few things to hold onto before you teach:

- **The line between the wise and foolish builder is NOT knowledge.** Both builders heard Jesus' words. The line is *practice.* Hearing plus doing = rock. Hearing minus doing = sand.
- **The storm is a given.** Jesus doesn't say *if* the rain falls — he says *when.* Every life gets storms. Middle school has plenty already; adulthood brings more. The question is never whether the rain comes. It's always what your house is built on.
- **Verse 28–29 is the crowd's reaction.** *"When Jesus had finished saying these things, the crowds were amazed at his teaching, because he taught as one who had authority, and not as their teachers of the law."* Jesus didn't just quote other rabbis. He spoke with the authority of the one these words came from in the first place.

## Prop Department
*What supplies do I need?*

- Bibles, one per student
- **A Jenga tower** (or Lego bricks, or a small stack of books) — for the building demo
- Whiteboard or large paper + markers
- Index cards — one per student
- Pens/pencils
- Christ light / candle + matches (for opening)
- Optional: a speaker + "New Way to Be Human" (Switchfoot) cued up for the close

## Directing the Scene
*What learning activities might we do?*

### Start with Prayer

Light the Christ light. Invite a student to lead in prayer. This is the last lesson of a nine-week series — ask God to help us not just *finish* the Sermon on the Mount, but *build our lives* on it.

### Catch Their Attention — The Building Test

**Before the lesson:**
Have a Jenga tower built and ready on the table. (If you don't have Jenga, stack books, Legos, or Solo cups.)

**During the lesson:**
Gesture at the tower. Say:
> This tower is solid. Nobody's touching it. But — what would happen if I started pulling pieces from the *bottom*?

Pull a block from the bottom row. Then another. Keep going until it wobbles. Let a student knock it down if they want.

Then rebuild a tower and ask:
> What if instead we pulled pieces from the *top*? Very different story, right?
>
> What everything is built *on* matters more than what's on top. Tonight — on our last night in the Sermon on the Mount — Jesus is going to tell us a story about exactly that.

### Read and Interpret — Matthew 7:24–29

**Intro for the youth:** Before reading, say:
> We've been climbing this mountain for eight weeks. We heard who God calls blessed. We got called salt and light. We got pushed past the rules to the heart. We learned how to pray. We got warned about trying to serve two masters. We learned how to trust instead of worry. We got told to check our own plank before we go after anyone's speck. We heard that our Father loves to be asked. Tonight Jesus wraps it all up. Listen for the moment the whole series comes to a point.

Read **Matthew 7:24–29** together. Go around the circle. Read it slowly.

**Discuss:**

1. ***What's the ONE difference between the wise builder and the foolish builder?*** — Both hear Jesus' words. Only one *puts them into practice.*
2. ***Do both houses get the storm?*** — Yes. Jesus doesn't say *if.* He says *when.*
3. ***What are the rains, streams, and winds in real life?*** — Middle school drama. Family stuff. Losses. Anxiety. The hard year. The heartbreak. The thing that blindsides you.
4. ***Why does the wise builder's house stand?*** — Because of what it's *built on.* The foundation.
5. ***The foolish builder isn't evil. He's not a pagan. He heard Jesus' same words. What's his problem?*** — He never *did* anything about them. He filed them away. Information without action.
6. ***Look at verses 28–29. What's the crowd's reaction when Jesus finishes the whole sermon?*** — They're amazed. Because Jesus doesn't quote other teachers. He speaks like *he's* the one the words come from.

**Say:**
> Here's the punchline Jesus chose to close with. Not a bow. Not a blessing. A parable with teeth.
>
> **Everybody builds on something.** Grades. Sports. Looks. Friends. Followers. Money. Achievement. Parents' approval. Being funny. All of those things can be *good* — but not one of them can hold a life together when the storm rolls in.
>
> And Jesus isn't saying "listen to me, memorize me, share me, post about me." He's saying *hear my words AND do them.* Hearing without doing is sand. Hearing plus doing is rock.
>
> That's the whole series in one sentence: **Jesus isn't asking us to try harder at religion. He's inviting us to build our whole life on him.**

### Series Recap — Eight Weeks in Ninety Seconds

Before Ponder, flash back through the series fast on the whiteboard. Write each one up as you say it:

- **Lesson 1 — Up the Mountain.** Jesus calls *who* blessed? Not who the world calls blessed. The poor in spirit, the mourners, the meek, the merciful, the peacemakers, the persecuted.
- **Lesson 2 — Salt and Light.** *You ARE salt. You ARE light.* Your life is supposed to flavor and illuminate the world.
- **Lesson 3 — But I Say.** Jesus doesn't just want your behavior. He wants your heart.
- **Lesson 4 — When You Pray.** You have a Father. Talk to him like a kid. Short. Honest. Trusting.
- **Lesson 5 — Treasures and Two Masters.** Whatever has your heart has your life. You can't serve God and money — pick one.
- **Lesson 6 — Do Not Worry.** Your Father feeds sparrows. He's got you. Seek first his kingdom.
- **Lesson 7 — Judge Not.** Check your own plank before you go after anyone's speck.
- **Lesson 8 — Ask, Seek, Knock.** Your Father loves to be asked. Treat people the way you want to be treated.

Point back at the Jenga tower.

> Every one of those isn't a brick to balance on top. They're the foundation. That's what Jesus is telling us to build on.

### Ponder the Possibilities

Discuss together:

1. ***Of the eight earlier lessons, which one has been hardest for you to actually put into practice?***
2. ***What are some things middle schoolers try to build their lives on — sand that looks solid until the storm comes?***
3. ***Has a storm ever hit you and you realized you were building on sand? What happened?***
4. ***Jesus is super clear: hearing without doing is not enough. Is there something from these nine weeks you've *heard* but not actually *done*?***
5. ***What would it look like to build your middle-school life on Jesus? Not someday. This week.***

### Turn Toward Action — What Are You Building On?

Hand each student an index card. Say:
> Last Sermon on the Mount homework of the series. Three questions. Be honest.

1. **What am I building my life on *right now*?** (The honest answer. Not the Sunday-school answer.)
2. **What do I *want* to be building on?**
3. **ONE thing I'll do this week to lay a brick on Jesus.** (Pray the Lord's Prayer every morning. Reconcile with that person. Refuse to join that gossip chain. Hand that worry to God. Say hi to that kid nobody sits with.)

Keep the card. Put it in your Bible, on your mirror, in your locker, in your phone case.

**Close together.** If you have the speaker cued up, play "New Way to Be Human" by Switchfoot as students finish writing. Pray together:

> Jesus, we don't want to build on sand. We've heard you for nine weeks. Help us *do* you for the next sixty years. Thank you for showing us a new way to be human. Make us wise builders. Make our lives stand on you. Amen.

Stand at the door as students leave. Say each student's name and bless them out:

> *"[Name], you've been loved, you've belonged, and you've been made to make a difference. Now go build on the rock."*
