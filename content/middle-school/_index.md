**Grades 6–8.** The ages most adults pretend they forgot. The ages that, according to the Barna Group, are when nearly 64% of lifelong Christians first made a commitment to Christ. Coincidence? Probably not.

These lessons are written for the real middle schooler — the one who laughs too loud, then goes quiet when it matters, then asks a question that'll make you rethink your whole theology on the car ride home.

## What to expect from an MS lesson

Every lesson on this site follows the same template so volunteers aren't left guessing:

- **Behind the Curtain** — what the teacher needs to know before walking in
- **Center Stage** — the ONE thing you want students to walk out with
- **Prop Department** — the shopping list
- **Directing the Scene** — the actual plan: prayer, hook, Scripture, discussion, and a tangible "turn toward action"

{{< callout type="info" >}}
All MS lessons target ~45–60 minutes and are written assuming a group of 6–15 students, family-style. Scale the activities to your room.
